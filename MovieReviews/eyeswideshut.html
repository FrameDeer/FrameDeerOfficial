Eyes Wide Shut (1999)

Genre: Drama, Mystery, Thriller | Runtime: 159 min | Director: Stanley Kubrick

After his wife reveals a shocking sexual fantasy, a New York City doctor embarks on a night-long odyssey of sexual and emotional discovery. Dr. Bill Harford's journey takes him through a series of increasingly surreal encounters, culminating in a mysterious masked orgy that threatens to unravel his entire existence.

FrameDeer Thoughts

Stanley Kubrick's final masterpiece is a haunting exploration of marriage, sexuality, and the hidden desires that lie beneath the surface of civilized society. Eyes Wide Shut represents the culmination of Kubrick's career-long fascination with human psychology and social structures.

Tom Cruise delivers one of his most nuanced performances as Dr. Bill Harford, a man whose comfortable existence is shattered by his wife's admission of sexual fantasy. Nicole Kidman is equally compelling as Alice, whose casual revelation sets in motion a chain of events that exposes the fragility of their marriage.

The film's dreamlike quality is established through Kubrick's meticulous direction, with every frame composed with painterly precision. The Christmas setting, with its twinkling lights and festive decorations, creates a stark contrast to the dark psychological journey unfolding beneath the surface.

What makes Eyes Wide Shut particularly fascinating is how it operates on multiple levels simultaneously. It's both a straightforward narrative about a man's nocturnal adventures and a complex allegory about the masks we wear in society. The famous orgy sequence remains one of cinema's most discussed and analyzed scenes.

The supporting cast, including Sydney Pollack as Bill's confidant and the various characters Bill encounters on his journey, add layers of mystery and intrigue. Jocelyn Pook's haunting score perfectly complements the film's unsettling atmosphere.

Final verdict: A challenging, hypnotic, and deeply philosophical work that rewards multiple viewings. Kubrick's swan song is a fitting conclusion to one of cinema's most brilliant careers.

The Making of Eyes Wide Shut

Stanley Kubrick's final film represents the culmination of his legendary career. Based on Arthur Schnitzler's 1926 novella "Dream Story," Kubrick spent nearly 30 years developing the project, considering it his most personal and important work.

The production became infamous for its unprecedented 400-day shooting schedule, one of the longest continuous shoots in film history. Kubrick's legendary perfectionism was on full display, with some scenes requiring over 100 takes. The famous walk through New York streets was actually filmed on a massive set at Pinewood Studios, with Kubrick meticulously recreating Greenwich Village.

The controversial orgy sequence required extreme secrecy and security. Kubrick employed body doubles and digital manipulation to satisfy the MPAA's rating requirements while maintaining his artistic vision. The elaborate masks and costumes were designed to create an atmosphere of aristocratic decadence and menace.

Tragically, Kubrick died just six days after presenting his final cut to Warner Bros., never seeing the film's theatrical release. The film stands as his final statement on the human condition - a haunting exploration of marriage, jealousy, and the dark undercurrents of desire that flow beneath civilized society.

Fun Facts & Trivia

  • Kubrick demanded total secrecy - the script was printed on red paper to prevent photocopying
  • Tom Cruise and Nicole Kidman were married during production but divorced before release
  • The film holds the Guinness World Record for longest continuous film shoot
  • Kubrick edited the film himself, working 18-hour days in the final months
  • The famous orgy scene required 200 extras and was shot over several weeks
