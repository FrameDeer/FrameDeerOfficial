Red Notice (2021)

Genre: Action, Comedy, Thriller | Runtime: 118 min | Director: Rawson Marshall Thurber

An Interpol agent tracks the world's most wanted art thief around the globe in a high-stakes game of cat and mouse. When a daring heist pulls them into a plot to steal one of Cleopatra's priceless bejeweled eggs, the agent is forced to team up with a brilliant con artist to catch the elusive thief who's always one step ahead. The trio engages in a globe-trotting adventure filled with twists, double-crosses, and explosive action as they race to recover the legendary artifacts before they're lost forever.

FrameDeer Thoughts

Rawson Marshall Thurber's big-budget Netflix heist film represents the peak of streaming service blockbuster filmmakingâ€”high on star power and production values, but lacking in originality and genuine excitement. Red Notice feels like a carefully calculated product designed to appeal to the broadest possible audience rather than an authentic creative vision.

The film's strongest asset is undoubtedly the chemistry between its three leads. Dwayne Johnson and Ryan Reynolds have an easygoing rapport that delivers some genuinely amusing moments, though their banter often feels recycled from better films. Gal Gadot brings her signature charisma to the role of the world's greatest art thief, though the script doesn't give her much beyond being effortlessly cool and always one step ahead.

The action sequences are competently executed but lack the inventiveness and visceral impact that might elevate the material. From prison breaks to museum heists to jungle chases, every set piece feels like a polished but generic version of something we've seen done better elsewhere. The CGI-heavy approach removes much of the tension and physicality that makes great action cinema compelling.

Where Red Notice truly struggles is in its predictable plotting and over-reliance on twists that fail to surprise. The film constantly telegraphs its reveals while simultaneously trying to convince audiences they're witnessing clever storytelling. The globetrotting adventure lacks a sense of genuine danger or stakes, making the expensive spectacle feel weightless.

Final verdict: A perfectly serviceable but ultimately forgettable action-comedy that plays it too safe. While the star power provides some entertainment value, Red Notice fails to justify its massive budget or leave any lasting impression beyond being Netflix's most-watched movie at the time of release.

The Making of Red Notice

Red Notice represents Netflix's ambitious push into blockbuster filmmaking, with a budget reportedly around $200 million making it one of the most expensive Netflix original films ever produced. The film was part of Netflix's strategy to compete with traditional studio tentpoles by combining A-list stars with global action-adventure storytelling.

The production faced significant challenges due to the COVID-19 pandemic, which forced shutdowns and complicated international filming. Originally planned as a Universal Pictures release, the film was acquired by Netflix in 2019 as part of a massive deal that reflected the streaming service's commitment to big-budget, star-driven content.

The casting of the three leads was a major coup for Netflix, bringing together three of Hollywood's most bankable stars. Dwayne Johnson's Seven Bucks Productions was heavily involved in the development, marking his continued collaboration with director Rawson Marshall Thurber following their work on "Central Intelligence" and "Skyscraper."

Despite the film's globetrotting premise, much of the production was affected by pandemic restrictions. The crew had to get creative with locations, using Atlanta, Georgia to stand in for various international settings and relying heavily on CGI to create the illusion of worldwide travel. The production's scale and the stars' massive salaries accounted for the enormous budget.
