Focus (2015)

Genre: Crime, Drama, Romance | Runtime: 105 min | Directors: Glenn Ficarra, John Requa

A veteran con artist takes an aspiring femme fatale under his wing in the world of international grifters. Nicky Spurgeon (Will Smith) is a master of deception who meets Jess Barrett (Margot Robbie), an ambitious newcomer to the con game. As Nicky teaches Jess the tricks of the trade, their professional partnership turns into a romantic one, complicating their already dangerous lifestyle. When the stakes get higher with a billionaire race team owner as their mark, Nicky and Jess must navigate a world where nothing is as it seems and no one can be trusted—not even each other.

FrameDeer Thoughts

Focus delivers slick entertainment with style and charm, even if it doesn't reinvent the con artist genre. Will Smith returns to his charismatic roots as Nicky Spurgeon, a seasoned grifter who's as smooth with his words as he is with his cons.

The film's strongest asset is the electric chemistry between Will Smith and Margot Robbie, who previously worked together in The Wolf of Wall Street. Their mentor-protégé relationship evolves into a complex romantic dynamic that keeps the audience guessing about everyone's true motives. The Buenos Aires and New Orleans settings provide a glamorous backdrop for the high-stakes cons.

The con sequences are cleverly executed, particularly the extended Super Bowl sequence that serves as the film's centerpiece. The attention to detail in showing how complex grifts work gives the film an authentic feel, even when the twists become increasingly elaborate. The directors Ficarra and Requa bring the same sharp dialogue and character work they demonstrated in Crazy, Stupid, Love.

Where Focus occasionally stumbles is in its third-act twists, which some viewers may find overly convoluted. However, the film maintains its entertainment value through strong performances and stylish direction. Rodrigo Santoro delivers a solid supporting performance as the wealthy mark, while Gerald McRaney adds gravitas as the security expert.

The film successfully captures the glamorous, jet-setting lifestyle of international con artists while never losing sight of the emotional stakes. It's a fun, stylish ride that doesn't take itself too seriously.

Final verdict: A stylish, entertaining con artist film powered by strong chemistry between its leads. While not groundbreaking, it delivers exactly what it promises—a good time with plenty of twists and charm.

The Making of Focus

Focus marked the second collaboration between Will Smith and Margot Robbie, following their work together in The Wolf of Wall Street. The chemistry they developed on that film was a key factor in their casting for Focus, with the directors specifically writing the roles with them in mind.

The film's elaborate con sequences required extensive research into real-life grifting techniques. The production consulted with former con artists to ensure the schemes depicted were both plausible and authentic. The Super Bowl sequence, which serves as the film's centerpiece, was particularly complex to stage and required coordination with the NFL for authenticity.

Filming took place in multiple glamorous locations, including New Orleans and Buenos Aires, to create the international jet-set atmosphere central to the story. The production design team worked to create distinct visual styles for each location, from the vibrant energy of Buenos Aires to the sophisticated elegance of New York high society.

Will Smith underwent training with professional pickpockets and con artists to prepare for his role, learning the subtle techniques and psychological manipulation used in real grifts. Margot Robbie similarly trained to master the physicality and confidence required for her character's transformation from novice to skilled con artist.
