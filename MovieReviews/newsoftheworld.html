News of the World (2020)

Genre: Western, Drama, Adventure | Runtime: 118 min | Director: Paul Greengrass

Five years after the Civil War, Captain Jefferson Kyle Kidd (Tom Hanks), a widowed veteran and itinerant newsreader, travels from town to town in Texas, bringing the news of the world to local populations for a small fee. On his travels, he encounters Johanna (Helena Zengel), a 10-year-old girl who was taken in by the Kiowa people six years earlier and has just been recovered by the U.S. Army. With no living relatives willing to take her, Kidd agrees to deliver her to her aunt and uncle near San Antonio. Their journey through the dangerous and unsettled Texas landscape becomes a test of survival as they face natural disasters, hostile encounters, and their own emotional barriers. As they travel together, the gruff newsreader and the traumatized child form an unlikely bond that helps them both heal from their respective losses and find new purpose in a rapidly changing America.

FrameDeer Thoughts

Paul Greengrass's thoughtful Western represents a departure from his signature kinetic action style, instead delivering a measured, character-driven story about connection and healing in post-Civil War America. News of the World combines the epic scale of classic Westerns with intimate character study, creating a film that feels both grand in scope and deeply personal in its emotional resonance.

Tom Hanks delivers one of his most nuanced performances as Captain Kidd, perfectly capturing the weariness and dignity of a man trying to make sense of a fractured nation while carrying his own emotional burdens. His portrayal of a newsreader who understands the power of stories to unite and heal adds layers of thematic depth to what could have been a straightforward Western narrative. Helena Zengel is a revelation as Johanna, delivering a remarkably expressive performance with minimal dialogue. Her ability to convey complex emotions through physicality and facial expressions creates a character who is simultaneously fierce, vulnerable, and utterly compelling.

The film's visual language is breathtaking, with cinematographer Dariusz Wolski capturing the rugged beauty of the Texas landscape in all its harsh majesty. The careful composition and natural lighting create a sense of authenticity that grounds the story in its historical context. James Newton Howard's score perfectly complements the visuals, blending traditional Western themes with more intimate, emotional motifs that underscore the developing bond between the two main characters.

What makes News of the World particularly compelling is its timely exploration of truth, storytelling, and national healing. The film uses Kidd's profession as a newsreader to comment on the importance of shared narratives in bringing divided communities togetherâ€”a theme that resonates strongly in our current era of information fragmentation. The relationship between Kidd and Johanna becomes a microcosm of this larger theme, as they must learn to bridge their cultural and linguistic divides to survive.

Final verdict: A beautifully crafted, emotionally resonant Western that showcases two outstanding performances and offers thoughtful commentary on storytelling's power to heal. While it may move at a more deliberate pace than some modern Westerns, News of the World rewards patient viewers with rich character development, stunning visuals, and a deeply moving exploration of human connection in troubled times.

The Making of News of the World

The film is based on Paulette Jiles' 2016 novel of the same name, which director Paul Greengrass discovered and immediately saw cinematic potential in. Greengrass, known for his intense action films like the Bourne series, was drawn to the story's more contemplative nature and its themes of connection and healing in a divided nation. He worked with screenwriter Luke Davies to adapt the novel, focusing on the central relationship between Kidd and Johanna while maintaining the historical authenticity of the post-Civil War setting.

Casting Helena Zengel was crucial to the film's success. The young German actress was discovered by Greengrass after he saw her performance in "System Crasher," for which she won the Silver Bear at Berlin International Film Festival. Despite the language barrier (Zengel spoke little English at the time), her remarkable ability to convey emotion without dialogue made her perfect for the role of Johanna. She worked intensively with language coaches to learn her English lines and Kiowa dialogue, while Tom Hanks helped create a supportive environment on set.

The production faced the challenge of filming in New Mexico's challenging terrain during the fall and early winter of 2019. Cinematographer Dariusz Wolski used natural light whenever possible to create the film's authentic, dusty aesthetic. The production design team meticulously recreated 1870s Texas, from the small frontier towns to the rugged wilderness. Particular attention was paid to the historical accuracy of Kidd's wagon and newsreading equipment, as well as the costumes that reflected the period's hardships.

The film's release was significantly impacted by the COVID-19 pandemic, with its theatrical run limited and much of its audience seeing it through video on demand. Despite these challenges, it received critical acclaim for its performances, cinematography, and thoughtful storytelling, earning four Oscar nominations and establishing itself as one of the standout films of the pandemic era.
