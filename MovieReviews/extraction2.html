Extraction 2 (2023)

Genre: Action, Thriller | Runtime: 122 min | Director: Sam Hargrave

After barely surviving the events of the first movie, Tyler Rake is back, and his team is ready for another mission. Tasked with extracting a family from a heavily fortified Georgian prison, Rake finds himself in another deadly situation. The mission becomes more complicated when he discovers the family is being targeted by a powerful crime lord. With non-stop action and incredible stunts, Rake must use all his skills to complete the extraction and survive against overwhelming odds.

FrameDeer Thoughts

Sam Hargrave delivers another adrenaline-fueled action spectacle that pushes the boundaries of practical stunt work and continuous-shot action sequences. Extraction 2 builds upon the foundation of its predecessor while significantly raising the stakes and technical execution.

The film's centerpiece—a 21-minute continuous-shot action sequence—stands as one of the most impressive technical achievements in modern action cinema. From prison riot to car chase to train extraction, this breathtaking sequence showcases Hargrave's background as a stunt coordinator and his mastery of practical filmmaking.

Chris Hemsworth solidifies his status as an action star with a physically demanding performance that sees him performing many of his own stunts. His portrayal of Tyler Rake evolves beyond the tormented mercenary archetype, showing glimpses of emotional depth and moral complexity while maintaining the brutal efficiency that made the character iconic.

The supporting cast elevates the material, with Golshifteh Farahani returning as Nik Khan and adding both combat prowess and emotional grounding to the narrative. The family dynamics at the heart of the extraction mission provide genuine emotional stakes that complement the relentless action.

Where Extraction 2 truly excels is in its commitment to practical effects and visceral combat choreography. The fight scenes are brutal, inventive, and shot with clarity that allows viewers to appreciate the incredible stunt work. The prison sequence alone justifies the film's existence, featuring some of the most creative close-quarters combat in recent memory.

Final verdict: A masterclass in action filmmaking that delivers exactly what it promises—relentless, well-choreographed, technically impressive set pieces that will satisfy any action enthusiast.

The Making of Extraction 2

Sam Hargrave's vision for Extraction 2 was to push the boundaries of what audiences expect from action cinema, particularly in the streaming space. Building on the success of the first film, Hargrave and his team aimed to create even more ambitious action sequences while maintaining the gritty, practical approach that defined the original.

The film's legendary 21-minute continuous-shot sequence required months of meticulous planning and rehearsal. The sequence begins with a prison riot, moves through multiple floors of combat, transitions to a car chase, and culminates in a train extraction. Each segment was choreographed to perfection, with hidden cuts seamlessly integrated to maintain the illusion of a single continuous take. Chris Hemsworth and the stunt team trained for weeks to execute the complex physical requirements.

The prison set construction was a massive undertaking, built across multiple soundstages to allow for the continuous camera movement. Special camera rigs were designed to follow the action through tight corridors, over obstacles, and between vehicles. The train sequence alone required building a functional train car on a gimbal that could simulate high-speed movement while allowing for safe stunt execution.

Hargrave's background as a stunt coordinator proved invaluable, as he prioritized actor safety while demanding authentic, visceral action. The fight choreography emphasizes practical techniques and real martial arts, with minimal wire work or CGI enhancement. This commitment to realism gives the action sequences a weight and impact rarely seen in modern blockbusters.
