Bohemian Rhapsody (2018)

Genre: Biography, Drama, Music | Runtime: 134 min | Director: Bryan Singer

A chronicle of the years leading up to Queen's legendary appearance at the Live Aid (1985) concert. The film follows the meteoric rise of the band through their iconic songs and revolutionary sound, their near-implosion as Mercury's lifestyle spirals out of control, and their triumphant reunion on the eve of Live Aid. At the center of the story is Freddie Mercury, a man with a dream and an extraordinary voice who defies stereotypes and conventions to become one of the most beloved entertainers on the planet.

FrameDeer Thoughts

Rami Malek's transformative performance as Freddie Mercury is nothing short of extraordinary in Bohemian Rhapsody. The film captures the essence of Queen's meteoric rise and the complex, brilliant man at its center.

While the film takes some creative liberties with the band's actual timeline, it succeeds in conveying the revolutionary spirit of Queen's music and Freddie's unparalleled stage presence. The recreation of Live Aid is a cinematic achievement that alone makes the film worth watching.

The supporting cast delivers strong performances, particularly Gwilym Lee as Brian May and Ben Hardy as Roger Taylor, who capture the musical chemistry and brotherly dynamics of the band. The film beautifully portrays the creative process behind some of rock's most iconic songs.

Where Bohemian Rhapsody truly shines is in its musical sequences. The painstaking recreation of Queen's recording sessions and live performances transports viewers back to the golden age of stadium rock. The sound mixing and editing deservedly won Academy Awards.

The film handles Freddie's personal struggles with sensitivity, though some critics argue it could have delved deeper into his complexities. What remains undeniable is the emotional impact of his journey and the enduring power of Queen's music.

Final verdict: A crowd-pleasing tribute to one of music's greatest frontmen and bands. While not a perfect biopic, it's an entertaining and emotionally resonant celebration of Queen's legacy.

The Making of Bohemian Rhapsody

Development of Bohemian Rhapsody spanned nearly a decade, with Sacha Baron Cohen originally attached to play Freddie Mercury before creative differences led to his departure. The project went through multiple directors and script revisions before finally moving forward with Rami Malek and Bryan Singer.

Rami Malek underwent extensive preparation for his role as Freddie Mercury. He worked with movement coaches to master Mercury's distinctive stage presence, spent hours studying concert footage, and wore prosthetic teeth to replicate Freddie's famous overbite. The dental pieces significantly affected his speech, requiring additional vocal training.

The Live Aid recreation was filmed over seven days at Bovingdon Airfield in Hertfordshire, where a massive set was built to replicate Wembley Stadium. The sequence was shot in one continuous take for the first song, capturing the energy and scale of Queen's legendary performance. Surviving Queen members Brian May and Roger Taylor served as consultants throughout production.

The film's musical sequences used a combination of Malek's own vocals, recordings of Canadian singer Marc Martel (known for his uncanny resemblance to Mercury's voice), and original Freddie Mercury recordings. The sound team seamlessly blended these elements to create the film's authentic Queen sound.
