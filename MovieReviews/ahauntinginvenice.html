A Haunting in Venice (2023)

Genre: Mystery, Horror, Thriller | Runtime: 103 min | Director: Kenneth Branagh

Now retired and living in self-imposed exile in Venice, Italy, the famous detective Hercule Poirot (Kenneth Branagh) reluctantly attends a seance in a haunted palazzo on All Hallows' Eve. When one of the guests is murdered, Poirot is thrust back into a world of mystery and intrigue that challenges both his detective skills and his skepticism of the supernatural. Set against the backdrop of post-World War II Venice during a flooding aqua alta, the film follows Poirot as he investigates a cast of suspicious characters, including a psychic medium (Michelle Yeoh), an opera singer (Tina Fey), and various family members with dark secrets. As supernatural occurrences escalate, Poirot must determine if he's dealing with human evil or something truly otherworldly.

FrameDeer Thoughts

Kenneth Branagh's third Hercule Poirot installment marks a significant departure from the previous films, embracing gothic horror elements while maintaining the classic mystery structure that Agatha Christie fans expect. A Haunting in Venice successfully blends supernatural tension with rational detective work, creating the most atmospheric and visually striking entry in Branagh's Christie adaptations.

The film's greatest achievement lies in its masterful atmosphere-building. Cinematographer Haris Zambarloukos creates a visually stunning palette of shadows and reflections, using Venice's watery landscape to enhance the sense of unease. The palazzo setting becomes a character in itselfâ€”a decaying, flooded mansion filled with secrets and supernatural possibilities. The decision to shoot primarily on practical locations in Venice pays off tremendously, lending authenticity to the eerie atmosphere.

Kenneth Branagh delivers his most nuanced performance as Poirot to date, portraying the detective as older, wearier, and grappling with his own mortality and skepticism. His Poirot is a man haunted by past cases and the trauma of war, making his confrontation with potential supernatural forces particularly compelling. The film cleverly uses the character's famous rationality as a foil for the unexplained events.

The supporting cast shines in their respective roles. Michelle Yeoh brings gravitas and mystery as psychic Joyce Reynolds, while Tina Fey provides sharp wit as mystery novelist Ariadne Oliver. The younger cast members, including Jude Hill and Kyle Allen, hold their own against the veteran actors, creating a believable ensemble of suspects with complex motivations.

The horror elements are handled with sophistication, relying more on psychological tension and atmospheric dread than cheap jump scares. The seance sequence stands out as a masterclass in building suspense, while the supernatural occurrences genuinely challenge both Poirot and the audience's perceptions of reality.

Final verdict: A successful genre-bending experiment that revitalizes the Christie adaptation format. A Haunting in Venice delivers both a satisfying mystery and a genuinely creepy gothic horror experience, making it the strongest and most ambitious entry in Branagh's Poirot trilogy.

The Making of A Haunting in Venice

Kenneth Branagh's vision for the third Poirot film was to create a significant departure from the previous installments, moving from the lavish, sun-drenched settings of Murder on the Orient Express and Death on the Nile to the shadowy, supernatural atmosphere of post-war Venice. Inspired by Agatha Christie's lesser-known Halloween Party, Branagh and screenwriter Michael Green reimagined the story as a gothic horror mystery that would challenge Poirot's rational worldview.

The production faced the unique challenge of filming in actual Venetian palazzos during the aqua alta (high water) season. Cinematographer Haris Zambarloukos used the city's natural flooding to create haunting reflections and a sense of pervasive dampness and decay. The team employed innovative lighting techniques to enhance the supernatural atmosphere, using candlelight and practical effects to create the seance sequences rather than relying solely on digital effects.

The cast preparation involved intensive rehearsals to build the ensemble chemistry crucial for a Christie mystery. Michelle Yeoh studied real psychic mediums to prepare for her role, while Tina Fey worked with Branagh to develop the character of Ariadne Oliver as both comic relief and crucial plot catalyst. The younger actors, including Jude Hill (who previously worked with Branagh in Belfast), received special attention to ensure they could handle the film's darker themes.

Practical effects were prioritized throughout production, with the team building elaborate sets at Pinewood Studios that could be flooded to match the Venice locations. The supernatural elements were carefully designed to be ambiguous, allowing audiences to question whether they were witnessing real hauntings or elaborate tricksâ€”mirroring Poirot's own dilemma throughout the investigation.
