Genre: Action, Drama, Sports | Runtime: 132 min | Director: Joseph Kosinski

A veteran Formula 1 driver forced into retirement returns to the sport for one last shot at glory, mentoring a talented but reckless rookie while competing against his former team. Sonny Hayes (Brad Pitt), a former world champion who walked away from the sport after a tragic accident, is pulled back into the high-stakes world of Formula 1 racing. Teaming up with a promising young driver and an underdog team, Hayes must confront his past while competing against the very organization he helped build into a championship-winning powerhouse.

FrameDeer Thoughts

Joseph Kosinski delivers another technical masterpiece with F1, capturing the raw intensity and glamour of Formula 1 racing like never before. Following his success with Top Gun: Maverick, Kosinski brings the same level of practical filmmaking and visceral authenticity to the world of motorsports.

The film's racing sequences are nothing short of breathtaking. Using custom-built camera systems mounted directly on actual F1 cars during real Grand Prix weekends, the audience is placed directly in the cockpit at 200+ mph. The sound design alone deserves awards, capturing the scream of hybrid power units and the roar of crowds in stunning detail.

Brad Pitt delivers one of his most compelling performances in years as Sonny Hayes, a driver haunted by past mistakes but driven by unfinished business. His chemistry with co-star Damson Idris creates a compelling mentor-protégé dynamic that forms the emotional core of the film. The supporting cast, including Kerry Condon as the tough team principal, adds depth and authenticity to the racing world.

What sets F1 apart from previous racing films is its unprecedented access to the real Formula 1 world. Filmed during actual race weekends with cooperation from F1 teams and drivers, the film blends fiction with reality in a way that feels both authentic and cinematic. Lewis Hamilton's involvement as a producer ensures technical accuracy while maintaining dramatic tension.

The film balances high-octane action with genuine emotional stakes, exploring themes of redemption, legacy, and the price of greatness. It's both a love letter to motorsport and a compelling human drama that will appeal to racing enthusiasts and general audiences alike.

Final verdict: A technical and emotional tour de force that sets a new standard for sports films. Kosinski and Pitt have created a cinematic experience that demands to be seen on the biggest screen possible.

The Making of F1

F1 represents the most ambitious film project ever attempted in motorsports. Director Joseph Kosinski and producer Jerry Bruckheimer, fresh from their success with Top Gun: Maverick, partnered with seven-time world champion Lewis Hamilton to create an authentic portrayal of Formula 1 racing.

The production required unprecedented cooperation from Formula 1 Management and all ten F1 teams. Custom-built camera rigs were developed to mount cinema-quality cameras on actual F1 cars during real race weekends. Brad Pitt and Damson Idris underwent extensive racing training, spending months with professional drivers to learn proper racing techniques and physical conditioning.

The film's technical innovation extends to the creation of the fictional APXGP team. Rather than using CGI, the production built fully functional F2-level race cars that could be filmed alongside real F1 machinery. These cars were designed by actual F1 engineers and featured the same safety standards as championship vehicles.

Filming took place during actual Grand Prix weekends at Silverstone, Monaco, Spa-Francorchamps, and eight other circuits. The production had to work around the tight schedules of race weekends, often filming during practice sessions and capturing real race footage that would be integrated into the fictional narrative.
