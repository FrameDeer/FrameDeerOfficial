Gran Turismo (2023)

Genre: Action, Drama, Sports | Runtime: 135 min | Director: Neill Blomkamp

Based on the unbelievable true story, a team of underdogs - a working-class gamer (Archie Madekwe), a former race car driver (David Harbour), and an idealistic motorsport executive (Orlando Bloom) - risk it all to take on the most elite sport in the world. Jann Mardenborough (Archie Madekwe), a talented Gran Turismo player from Cardiff, Wales, wins a series of Nissan-sponsored video game competitions to become an actual professional race car driver. Under the mentorship of cynical former driver Jack Salter (David Harbour), Jann must prove that virtual racing skills can translate to the real world of high-speed, high-stakes professional motorsports. Based on the true story that shocked the racing world and proved that sometimes the best drivers come from the most unexpected places.

FrameDeer Thoughts

Neill Blomkamp delivers a surprisingly compelling and technically impressive racing drama that transcends its video game adaptation origins to become a genuinely inspiring underdog story. Gran Turismo succeeds where many video game movies fail by embracing the human drama at its core rather than relying solely on brand recognition, creating a film that works as both a sports drama and a character study.

The film's racing sequences are nothing short of spectacular, with Blomkamp employing his signature visual style to create some of the most immersive and visceral racing footage ever captured on film. Using a combination of practical effects, in-car cameras, and innovative CGI that seamlessly blends real racing footage with digital enhancements, the film puts viewers directly in the driver's seat. The attention to detail in recreating iconic tracks like the Nürburgring and showcasing the technical precision of professional racing is exceptional.

David Harbour delivers a standout performance as Jack Salter, the gruff, disillusioned former driver who becomes Jann's mentor. Harbour brings both toughness and vulnerability to the role, creating a character whose cynicism masks deep care for his protégé. His chemistry with Archie Madekwe forms the emotional core of the film, with their mentor-student relationship evolving in believable and moving ways throughout the narrative.

Archie Madekwe is convincing as Jann Mardenborough, capturing both the wide-eyed wonder of a gamer entering the professional racing world and the determination required to succeed at the highest level. His performance balances the character's natural talent with the very real fear and pressure of competing against seasoned professionals in one of the world's most dangerous sports.

The film's structure cleverly mirrors video game progression, with Jann advancing through increasingly difficult challenges, each requiring new skills and greater commitment. This narrative approach not only honors the source material but also creates natural dramatic tension as the stakes escalate from virtual competitions to real-life racing where mistakes can be fatal.

Final verdict: A thoroughly entertaining and surprisingly emotional sports drama that exceeds expectations. Gran Turismo combines breathtaking racing sequences with genuine human drama, creating an underdog story that will appeal to both racing enthusiasts and general audiences. While it follows familiar sports movie beats, the incredible true story and technical execution make it one of the better video game adaptations and a solid entry in the racing film genre.

The Making of Gran Turismo

Gran Turismo began as an unlikely project that transformed from a marketing idea into a major motion picture. The film is based on the true story of the GT Academy, a real-world competition created by Nissan and PlayStation that allowed top Gran Turismo players to compete for a chance to become professional race car drivers. The project went through several directors and development phases before Neill Blomkamp came on board, bringing his distinctive visual style to the racing sequences.

The film's innovative approach to racing cinematography involved extensive collaboration with real racing professionals and the actual Jann Mardenborough, who served as both a consultant and a stunt driver. The production used a combination of practical racing footage, custom-built camera rigs, and advanced CGI to create the immersive racing experience. Many of the racing sequences were filmed at actual Grand Prix tracks, including the Hungaroring in Hungary, which stood in for various international circuits.

Archie Madekwe's preparation for the role was extensive, involving both simulator training and real track experience to understand the physical and mental demands of professional racing. He worked closely with the real Jann Mardenborough to capture his mannerisms and racing style. Madekwe also underwent physical training to handle the G-forces and endurance required for the racing sequences, many of which were filmed in actual moving race cars.

The visual effects team faced the unique challenge of seamlessly blending practical racing footage with digital enhancements to create the film's signature "video game come to life" aesthetic. They developed new techniques for showing the digital racing line and other gaming elements that appear in the real-world racing scenes, creating a visual language that bridges the gap between simulation and reality.

David Harbour immersed himself in the world of motorsports, spending time with real racing coaches and former drivers to understand the mindset of someone like Jack Salter. His character, while fictional, represents the skepticism many in the racing world initially had about gamers transitioning to professional driving, making his eventual belief in Jann's potential more meaningful.
