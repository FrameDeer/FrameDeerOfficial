A Boy Called Christmas (2021)

Genre: Fantasy, Adventure, Family | Runtime: 106 min | Director: Gil Kenan

Eleven-year-old Nikolas (Henry Lawfull) lives in the woods of Finland with his father Joel (Michiel Huisman), a woodcutter struggling to provide for them after the loss of Nikolas' mother. When the king offers a reward to anyone who can bring back proof of the fabled elf village of Elfhelm, Joel joins an expedition, leaving Nikolas in the care of his cruel aunt. Determined to find his father, Nikolas sets off on an extraordinary adventure with his pet mouse Miika. Along the way, he encounters a reindeer named Blitzen, a truth-telling pixie, and discovers the magical hidden valley of Elfhelm, where he learns that hope and kindness are the greatest magic of all.

FrameDeer Thoughts

Gil Kenan's enchanting holiday film delivers a fresh and imaginative origin story for Santa Claus that feels both classic and contemporary. Based on Matt Haig's beloved book, A Boy Called Christmas combines heartfelt emotion, magical adventure, and just enough darkness to create a compelling Christmas tale that stands out in the crowded holiday movie landscape.

The film's greatest strength lies in its perfect balance of wonder and genuine emotional stakes. Henry Lawfull delivers a remarkable performance as young Nikolas, capturing both the character's innocence and his growing resilience. His journey from a grieving boy to the bringer of Christmas joy feels earned and emotionally resonant, avoiding the saccharine sentimentality that often plagues holiday films.

The magical world-building of Elfhelm is beautifully realized, with practical sets and visual effects that create a tangible, lived-in fantasy world. The elf society, with its intricate politics and cultural quirks, adds depth to what could have been a simple fairy tale. Maggie Smith's narration and performance as Aunt Ruth provides the perfect framing device, connecting the magical past to the present day.

The supporting cast elevates the material significantly. Zoe Colletti brings warmth and complexity to the truth-telling pixie Little Noosh, while Jim Broadbent adds gravitas as the conflicted Father Vodol. The dynamic between Nikolas and his pet mouse Miika provides both comic relief and emotional anchors throughout the adventure.

The film doesn't shy away from darker themesâ€”grief, loss, and disappointment are treated with respect, making the eventual triumph of hope and kindness feel genuinely meaningful. This emotional honesty, combined with stunning visuals and strong performances, creates a holiday film that appeals to both children and adults.

Final verdict: A magical, heartfelt origin story that deserves to become a Christmas classic. A Boy Called Christmas captures the true spirit of the holiday while telling a compelling adventure story with genuine emotional depth.

The Making of A Boy Called Christmas

The adaptation of Matt Haig's beloved book began when director Gil Kenan (Monster House, Poltergeist) fell in love with the story's unique approach to Santa Claus mythology. Kenan worked closely with Haig to preserve the book's emotional core while expanding the visual world for cinema. The production faced the unique challenge of creating a fresh take on Christmas origins while respecting established traditions.

The film's magical world was built through a combination of practical effects and digital enhancement. The elf village of Elfhelm was constructed as extensive practical sets in Czech studios, allowing the actors to interact with real environments. Director Kenan emphasized practical effects wherever possible, believing that tangible sets help young actors deliver more authentic performances.

Casting young Henry Lawfull as Nikolas was crucial to the film's success. After an extensive search, the filmmakers found Lawfull who brought both the innocence and determination needed for the role. His natural performance anchors the film's emotional journey. The supporting cast of established actors provided a strong foundation, with Maggie Smith's narration adding a layer of classic Christmas storytelling.

The production navigated the challenges of filming during the COVID-19 pandemic, implementing strict safety protocols while maintaining the film's magical atmosphere. Despite these challenges, the crew created elaborate sets including the entire elf village, Nikolas' woodland home, and the treacherous snowy landscapes of the journey north.
