Quentin Tarantino

Date of Birth: March 27, 1963

Location of Birth: Knoxville, Tennessee, USA

Height: 6'1" (185 cm)

Quentin Tarantino is an Academy Award-winning filmmaker known for his highly stylized, nonlinear narratives, sharp dialogue, and eclectic soundtrack choices. A video store clerk turned auteur, he revolutionized independent cinema in the 1990s and has created some of the most influential and quotable films of his generation.

Early Career

Quentin Tarantino dropped out of high school at age 15 and worked as an usher at a porn theater before landing a job at Video Archives in Manhattan Beach, California. During his five years working there, he immersed himself in film history and wrote his first scripts. His breakthrough came with Reservoir Dogs (1992), which he funded by selling his script for True Romance. The film's violent, talky style and nonlinear structure made it an instant cult classic and announced Tarantino as a major new voice in cinema. His follow-up, Pulp Fiction (1994), won the Palme d'Or and became a cultural phenomenon, establishing him as one of the most important filmmakers of his generation.

Signature Style & Themes

Nonlinear Storytelling

Chronologically fractured narratives that play with time and perspective to create suspense and revelation.

Pop Culture Dialogue

Characters engage in lengthy, meandering conversations about pop culture, food, and everyday minutiae.

Stylized Violence

Highly choreographed, often balletic violence that blends brutality with dark humor and visual poetry.

Eclectic Soundtracks

Curated soundtracks featuring surf rock, soul, spaghetti western scores, and obscure 60s/70s tracks.

Feet Fetish

Recurring close-up shots of women's bare feet throughout his filmography.

Genre Blending

Mixing and subverting multiple film genres within single narratives to create unique hybrid forms.
