Francis Ford Coppola

Date of Birth: April 7, 1939

Location of Birth: Detroit, Michigan, USA

Height: 6'1" (185 cm)

Nationality: American

Francis Ford Coppola is an American film director, producer, and screenwriter who emerged as a leading figure of the New Hollywood film movement of the 1970s. Best known for his epic Godfather trilogy and the Vietnam War masterpiece Apocalypse Now, Coppola has created some of the most influential and celebrated films in cinematic history while maintaining his status as an independent visionary and risk-taker.

Early Career

Coppola began his career working for B-movie producer Roger Corman, making his directorial debut with Dementia 13 (1963). He gained recognition as a screenwriter, winning his first Academy Award for Patton (1970). However, his breakthrough as a director came with The Godfather (1972), a film he initially didn't want to make but which became a cultural phenomenon and won him his first Best Picture Oscar. This success gave him the creative freedom to pursue more personal projects, establishing him as a central figure in the director-driven New Hollywood era alongside contemporaries like Martin Scorsese and Steven Spielberg.

Signature Style & Themes

Epic Family Sagas

Master of multi-generational storytelling that explores family dynamics, power, and corruption across time.

Moral Ambiguity

Exploration of complex moral dilemmas and the blurred lines between good and evil in human nature.

Operatic Scale

Grand, theatrical storytelling that combines intimate character drama with epic historical scope.

American Mythology

Examination of American dreams, ideals, and institutions through mythic storytelling frameworks.

Innovative Sound Design

Pioneering use of sound and music to create psychological depth and atmospheric tension.

Power & Corruption

Recurring exploration of how power transforms individuals and corrupts institutions and families.

Frequent Collaborators

Gordon Willis

Cinematographer known as "The Prince of Darkness" who shot the Godfather trilogy and The Conversation

Dean Tavoularis

Production designer on all Coppola films from The Godfather through The Rainmaker

Walter Murch

Sound designer and editor who revolutionized film sound on The Conversation and Apocalypse Now

Nino Rota

Composer who created the iconic Godfather theme and scored the first two films

Carmine Coppola

Father who composed scores for The Godfather Part II and Apocalypse Now

Vittorio Storaro

Cinematographer on Apocalypse Now and One from the Heart, creating visual masterpieces

Notable Trivia

American Zoetrope

Founded his independent production company to support innovative filmmakers and personal projects.

Wine Entrepreneur

Owns successful wineries in Napa Valley, using profits to finance his independent film projects.

Film School Rebel

Was nearly expelled from UCLA Film School for making "uncommercial" student films.

Apocalypse Now Chaos

The film's production became legendary for its disasters including typhoons, heart attacks, and mental breakdowns.

Digital Pioneer

Embraced digital filmmaking early, using new technologies for his later experimental works.

Family Dynasty

Father to filmmakers Sofia and Roman Coppola, and uncle to Nicolas Cage, creating a Hollywood dynasty.
