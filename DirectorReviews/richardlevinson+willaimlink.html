Richard Levinson & William Link

Richard Levinson: August 7, 1934 - March 12, 1987

William Link: December 21, 1933 - December 27, 2020

Location of Birth: Both born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, USA

Richard Levinson and William Link were one of television's most successful and influential writing partnerships, creating some of the most iconic detective series in television history. Known for their sophisticated mystery writing and character-driven storytelling, they revolutionized television crime drama with innovative concepts like the inverted mystery format in Columbo. Their collaboration spanned over three decades and produced numerous critically acclaimed series that continue to influence television writers today.

Early Career & Partnership

Levinson and Link met in junior high school in Philadelphia and began writing together as teenagers. They started by writing for radio and television anthology series in the 1950s, with their first major success coming from adapting their short story "Dear Corpus Delicti" into an episode of Alfred Hitchcock Presents in 1960. This story would later evolve into their most famous creation, Columbo. Their breakthrough came with the television movie "Prescription: Murder" (1968), which introduced Lieutenant Columbo and launched one of television's most enduring franchises. Throughout the 1970s and 1980s, they created a remarkable string of successful mystery series including Mannix, Ellery Queen, and Murder, She Wrote, establishing themselves as masters of the television mystery genre.

Signature Style & Innovations

Inverted Mystery Format

Pioneered the "howcatchem" structure in Columbo, showing the crime first and focusing on the detective's methodology.

Character-Driven Mysteries

Created detectives with distinctive personalities and quirks that became as important as the mysteries themselves.

Social Commentary

Skillfully wove relevant social issues into their mysteries without being preachy or sacrificing entertainment.

Intellectual Puzzles

Crafted sophisticated, logical mysteries that challenged viewers while remaining accessible and entertaining.

Collaborative Partnership

Maintained one of television's longest and most successful writing partnerships, complementing each other's strengths.

Television Format Mastery

Excelled at creating concepts perfectly suited for television's episodic structure and commercial breaks.

Frequent Collaborators

Peter Falk

The definitive Lieutenant Columbo, bringing their most famous creation to life with unparalleled authenticity

Angela Lansbury

Star of their creation Murder, She Wrote, embodying the amateur sleuth Jessica Fletcher for 12 seasons

Steve Bochco

Fellow television writer and producer who worked with them on various projects including Columbo

Dean Hargrove

Producer and writer who worked extensively on Columbo and other mystery series with the duo

Mike Connors

Star of Mannix, their successful private detective series that ran for eight seasons

Jim Hutton

Played the title role in their Ellery Queen series, bringing the classic detective to television

Notable Trivia & Legacy

High School Partnership

Began writing together as teenagers and maintained their partnership for over 40 years.

Columbo's Origin

Based the character on the detective from Crime and Punishment and a rumpled relative of Link's.

Social Pioneers

"That Certain Summer" was television's first sympathetic portrayal of homosexuality.

Writing Process

Typically worked in the same room, batting ideas back and forth until they reached consensus.

Industry Respect

Known as "the writers' writers" for their craftsmanship and integrity in Hollywood.

Enduring Influence

Their mystery formats continue to influence crime dramas and detective series worldwide.
