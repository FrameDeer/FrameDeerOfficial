Steven Spielberg

Date of Birth: December 18, 1946

Location of Birth: Cincinnati, Ohio, USA

Height: 5'7" (170 cm)

Nationality: American

Steven Spielberg is arguably the most influential filmmaker in cinema history, a visionary director, producer, and screenwriter who has defined modern blockbuster filmmaking while maintaining extraordinary artistic credibility. With a career spanning over five decades, he has created some of the most beloved and successful films of all time across virtually every genre.

Early Career

Spielberg began making amateur films at age 12 with his father's 8mm camera. His first professional directing credit came at Universal Studios where, as an unpaid intern, he directed the short film Amblin' (1968). This led to a television contract and his feature film debut with The Sugarland Express (1974). However, it was his second feature, Jaws (1975), that revolutionized Hollywood by creating the modern summer blockbuster and establishing Spielberg, at just 27 years old, as a major force in cinema. The film's unprecedented success set the stage for his legendary career.

Signature Style & Themes

Childlike Wonder

Master of capturing childhood innocence, imagination, and the sense of awe and discovery in extraordinary circumstances.

Family Dynamics

Recurring exploration of broken families, absent fathers, and the search for connection and belonging.

Ordinary in Extraordinary

Placing everyday characters in extraordinary situations, making epic stories relatable and emotionally resonant.

Fluid Camera Movement

Signature use of smooth, flowing camera work that guides the audience through emotional and narrative journeys.

Light as Character

Masterful use of light and shadow, particularly backlighting and lens flares, to create emotional and visual impact.

Historical Consciousness

Balancing entertainment with serious historical and moral themes, particularly in films about World War II.

Frequent Collaborators

John Williams

Composer on all but 5 of Spielberg's films since The Sugarland Express, creating iconic film scores

Kathleen Kennedy

Producer and longtime collaborator on over 30 films, co-founder of Amblin Entertainment

Janusz Kamiński

Cinematographer on all films since Schindler's List, creating distinctive visual style

Tom Hanks

Star of 5 films including Saving Private Ryan, Catch Me If You Can, and The Terminal

Frank Marshall

Producer and collaborator since Raiders of the Lost Ark, co-founder of Amblin Entertainment

Michael Kahn

Editor on nearly every Spielberg film since Close Encounters, winning 3 Academy Awards

Notable Trivia

Universal Intern

Sneaked onto the Universal Studios lot as a teenager and set up an unofficial office to learn filmmaking.

Youngest Billionaire Director

Became Hollywood's first billionaire filmmaker due to his share of box office revenues.

Amblin Entertainment

Founded his production company named after his first professional short film "Amblin'".

DreamWorks Co-founder

Co-founded DreamWorks SKG with Jeffrey Katzenberg and David Geffen in 1994.

Fear of Water

Has a fear of open water, which ironically made filming Jaws particularly challenging.

Video Game Pioneer

Consulted on and helped design some of the earliest video games in the 1980s.
