Álex Pina

Date of Birth: June 23, 1967

Location of Birth: Pamplona, Navarre, Spain

Height: 5'9" (175 cm)

Nationality: Spanish

Álex Pina is a Spanish television creator, writer, and producer who has revolutionized international television with his high-concept, adrenaline-fueled series. As the mastermind behind global phenomenon "Money Heist" (La Casa de Papel), Pina has become one of the most influential creators in the streaming era. His work is characterized by intricate plotting, morally complex characters, and relentless pacing that keeps audiences worldwide on the edge of their seats.

Early Career

Pina began his career as a journalist before transitioning to television writing in the late 1990s. He quickly made his mark with successful Spanish series like "Los hombres de Paco" and "El barco," developing his signature style of blending high-stakes drama with character-driven storytelling. His breakthrough came with creating the anti-hero centered series "Vis a Vis" (Locked Up), which demonstrated his ability to create compelling narratives around morally ambiguous characters. However, it was his creation of "Money Heist" in 2017 that would catapult him to international fame and redefine Spanish television's global impact.

Signature Style & Themes

High-Concept Thrillers

Master of creating irresistible premises that hook audiences from the first episode with impossible heists and high-stakes scenarios.

Moral Complexity

Specializes in creating anti-heroes and morally ambiguous characters that challenge traditional hero/villain dichotomies.

Non-linear Storytelling

Expert use of flashbacks, time jumps, and parallel narratives to build suspense and deepen character development.

Social Commentary

Weaves political and social themes into entertainment, exploring class struggle, institutional corruption, and rebellion.

Female Empowerment

Creates complex, powerful female characters who drive narratives and subvert traditional gender roles.

Cliffhanger Mastery

Unmatched ability to craft episode endings that leave audiences desperate for more, creating binge-worthy television.
