Alfred Hitchcock

Date of Birth: August 13, 1899

Location of Birth: Leytonstone, London, England

Date of Death: April 29, 1980

Height: 5'7" (170 cm)

Nationality: British (became US citizen in 1955)

Sir Alfred Hitchcock was an English film director, producer, and screenwriter widely regarded as one of the most influential filmmakers in the history of cinema. Known as the "Master of Suspense," he pioneered many techniques in the psychological thriller genre, creating a distinctive style that blended suspense, humor, and innovative visual storytelling across his 53 feature films spanning six decades.

Early Career

Hitchcock began his career in the British film industry in the early 1920s, working as a title card designer and art director. His directorial debut came with The Pleasure Garden (1925), but it was his ninth film, The Lodger: A Story of the London Fog (1927), that established his signature style and themes. His early British period produced classics like The 39 Steps (1935) and The Lady Vanishes (1938), which caught the attention of Hollywood producer David O. Selznick. Hitchcock moved to America in 1939, where he would create his most iconic works and become one of the most recognizable public figures in cinema history.

Signature Style & Techniques

The "MacGuffin"

Plot device that motivates the characters but ultimately proves unimportant to the overall story.

Suspense Over Surprise

Master of letting the audience know danger is coming, creating unbearable tension through anticipation.

Wrong Man Theme

Recurring plot of innocent protagonists caught in dangerous situations beyond their control.

Blonde Ice Queens

Signature female characters - elegant, sophisticated blondes with hidden emotional depths.

Camera as Character

Innovative camera movements and angles that create psychological tension and audience unease.

Cameo Appearances

His famous brief appearances in his own films became a trademark audiences eagerly anticipated.

Frequent Collaborators

Grace Kelly

Starred in three Hitchcock classics: Dial M for Murder, Rear Window, and To Catch a Thief

James Stewart

Collaborated on four films including Rear Window, Vertigo, and The Man Who Knew Too Much

Cary Grant

Starred in four films including North by Northwest, Notorious, and To Catch a Thief

Bernard Herrmann

Composer who created iconic scores for Psycho, Vertigo, North by Northwest, and more

Alma Reville

Wife and creative partner who worked as screenwriter and editor on many of his films

Robert Burks

Cinematographer on twelve consecutive Hitchcock films including all his 1950s classics

Notable Trivia

Fear of Eggs

Had ovophobia (fear of eggs), famously saying "I'm frightened of eggs, worse than frightened, they revolt me."

Never Won Best Director Oscar

Despite five nominations, he never won the Academy Award for Best Director.

Meticulous Planning

Believed "the film is made on the cutting room floor," planning every shot in advance through storyboards.

Television Personality

Hosted "Alfred Hitchcock Presents" (1955-1965), becoming a television icon with his dark humor.

Police Phobia

Had a lifelong fear of police stemming from being locked in a jail cell as a child by his father.

No Baths

Reportedly took only showers throughout his adult life, never baths.
