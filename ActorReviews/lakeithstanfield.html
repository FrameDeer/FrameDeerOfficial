LaKeith Stanfield

Date of Birth: August 12, 1991

Location of Birth: San Bernardino, California, USA

Height: 6'0" (183 cm)

LaKeith Stanfield is an Academy Award-nominated American actor and musician known for his unconventional character choices, magnetic screen presence, and remarkable versatility across independent films and major studio productions. Recognized for his ability to portray complex, often eccentric characters with depth and authenticity, he has established himself as one of the most distinctive and compelling actors of his generation.

Early Career

LaKeith Stanfield began his career with a breakthrough performance in the short film Short Term 12 (2008), which was later expanded into a feature film where he reprised his role. His portrayal of Marcus earned him critical attention and marked the beginning of his rise in independent cinema. He gained wider recognition for his role as Snoop Dogg in Straight Outta Compton (2015) and achieved mainstream success with his performance as Darius in the acclaimed television series Atlanta (2016-2022). His career reached new heights with his Academy Award-nominated performance in Judas and the Black Messiah (2021), cementing his status as a formidable talent capable of both subtle character work and powerful dramatic performances.
