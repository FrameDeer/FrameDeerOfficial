Natalie Portman

Date of Birth: June 9, 1981

Location of Birth: Jerusalem, Israel

Height: 5'3" (160 cm)

Natalie Portman is an Academy Award-winning actress known for her intelligent choice of roles, academic achievements, and remarkable versatility. From child star to respected actress and director, she has balanced a successful Hollywood career with academic pursuits at Harvard University, establishing herself as one of the most intellectually curious and talented actresses of her generation.

Early Career

Natalie Portman began her acting career at age 12, making her film debut in Léon: The Professional (1994), where her performance as a young girl taken in by a hitman garnered immediate critical attention. She continued to work as a teenager in films like Heat (1995) and Beautiful Girls (1996), but turned down more provocative roles to focus on her education. Her breakthrough came with Star Wars: Episode I – The Phantom Menace (1999), which made her an international star. Despite this massive success, she enrolled at Harvard University to study psychology, demonstrating her commitment to education. She continued acting during college breaks, appearing in the Star Wars prequels and making her Broadway debut in The Diary of Anne Frank. Her transition to adult roles was marked by challenging performances in independent films, culminating in her Academy Award-winning role in Black Swan (2010).

Major Awards

  • Academy Award for Best Actress – Black Swan (2011)
  • Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Black Swan (2011)
  • BAFTA Award for Best Actress – Black Swan (2011)
  • Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance – Black Swan (2011)
  • Academy Award nomination for Best Supporting Actress – Closer (2005)
  • Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actress – Closer (2005)
  • Critics' Choice Movie Award for Best Actress – Black Swan (2011)
  • Independent Spirit Award for Best Female Lead – Jackie (2017)
  • Saturn Award for Best Actress – Black Swan (2011)
  • Harvard University psychology degree (2003)
