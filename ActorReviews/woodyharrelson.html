Woody Harrelson

Date of Birth: July 23, 1961

Location of Birth: Midland, Texas, USA

Height: 5'10" (178 cm)

Nationality: American

Woody Harrelson is an Academy Award-nominated actor known for his versatile performances across comedy and drama. Rising to fame on the television series Cheers, he has since built an impressive film career spanning over three decades, with memorable roles in everything from blockbuster action films to intimate character studies.

Early Career

Woody Harrelson began his acting career in theater after graduating from Hanover College with a degree in theater arts and English. He moved to New York City and performed in off-Broadway productions before landing his breakthrough role as the naive bartender Woody Boyd on the hit NBC sitcom Cheers in 1985. He remained on the show for eight seasons, earning an Emmy Award for his performance. His film career started with small roles in movies like Wildcats (1986) and Cool Blue (1988), but his major film breakthrough came with Oliver Stone's Natural Born Killers (1994), which established him as a serious film actor beyond his television persona.

Major Awards & Nominations

  • Academy Award Nomination for Best Actor – The People vs. Larry Flynt (1997)
  • Academy Award Nomination for Best Supporting Actor – The Messenger (2010)
  • Academy Award Nomination for Best Supporting Actor – Three Billboards Outside Ebbing, Missouri (2018)
  • Golden Globe Nomination for Best Actor – The People vs. Larry Flynt (1997)
  • Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actor – Cheers (1989)
  • Screen Actors Guild Award – Three Billboards Outside Ebbing, Missouri (2018)
  • Independent Spirit Award for Best Supporting Male – The Messenger (2010)
