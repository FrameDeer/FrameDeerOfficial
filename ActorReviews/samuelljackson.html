Samuel L. Jackson

Date of Birth: December 21, 1948

Location of Birth: Washington, D.C., U.S.

Height: 6'2" (188 cm)

Samuel L. Jackson is one of the most prolific and recognizable actors in Hollywood history, holding the title of highest-grossing actor of all time. Known for his intense screen presence, distinctive voice, and frequent collaborations with director Quentin Tarantino, Jackson has created some of cinema's most memorable characters across a career spanning five decades.

Early Career

Samuel L. Jackson began his acting career in theater after studying at Morehouse College in Atlanta. He moved to New York in 1976 and spent years performing in off-Broadway productions while taking small film roles. His breakthrough came with Spike Lee's Jungle Fever (1991), for which he won a special award at Cannes. However, it was his role as Jules Winnfield in Quentin Tarantino's Pulp Fiction (1994) that catapulted him to international stardom, earning him an Academy Award nomination and establishing his signature intense, charismatic style. Throughout the 1990s, Jackson became one of Hollywood's most sought-after character actors with memorable roles in Die Hard with a Vengeance (1995), A Time to Kill (1996), and Jackie Brown (1997), often playing authoritative, no-nonsense characters with his trademark intensity.

Major Awards

  • Academy Award nomination for Best Supporting Actor – Pulp Fiction (1995)
  • BAFTA Award for Best Supporting Actor – Pulp Fiction (1995)
  • Cannes Film Festival Best Supporting Actor – Jungle Fever (1991)
  • Silver Bear for Best Actor – Jackie Brown (1998)
  • MTV Movie Award for Best On-Screen Duo – Pulp Fiction (1995)
  • NAACP Image Award for Outstanding Actor – Shaft (2001)
  • Guinness World Record for Highest-Grossing Actor of All Time
  • Hollywood Walk of Fame Star (2000)
  • Honorary Academy Award (2022)
  • Critics' Choice Movie Award for Best Acting Ensemble – Pulp Fiction (1995)
