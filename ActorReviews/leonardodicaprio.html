Leonardo DiCaprio

Date of Birth: November 11, 1974

Location of Birth: Los Angeles, California, USA

Height: 6'0" (183 cm)

Leonardo DiCaprio is an Academy Award-winning actor known for his intense roles in films ranging from blockbusters to character-driven dramas. With a career spanning over three decades, he has established himself as one of Hollywood's most versatile and dedicated performers.

Early Career

DiCaprio began his career in the late 1980s with appearances in television commercials and educational films. His breakthrough came in the early 1990s with a recurring role on the sitcom Growing Pains and his acclaimed performance as a homeless boy in This Boy's Life (1993). His career skyrocketed after his Oscar-nominated role as Arnie Grape in What's Eating Gilbert Grape (1993), establishing him as one of Hollywood's most promising young actors.

Major Awards

  • Academy Award for Best Actor – The Revenant (2016)
  • Golden Globe Award for Best Actor – The Wolf of Wall Street (2014)
  • Golden Globe Award for Best Actor – The Aviator (2005)
  • Golden Globe Award for Best Actor – The Revenant (2016)
  • Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Cast – The Departed (2007)
