Clint Eastwood

Date of Birth: May 31, 1930

Location of Birth: San Francisco, California, USA

Height: 6'4" (193 cm)

Clint Eastwood is an American cinematic icon who has achieved legendary status as both an actor and director. With a career spanning over seven decades, he is known for his minimalist acting style, distinctive voice, and powerful directorial vision. Eastwood has become one of Hollywood's most enduring and respected figures, transitioning from Western anti-hero to acclaimed filmmaker.

Early Career

Clint Eastwood began his career with small roles in B-movies and television in the 1950s. His breakthrough came with the role of Rowdy Yates in the CBS television series Rawhide (1959-1965). However, it was his collaboration with Italian director Sergio Leone in the "Dollars Trilogy" of Spaghetti Westerns—A Fistful of Dollars (1964), For a Few Dollars More (1965), and The Good, the Bad and the Ugly (1966)—that made him an international star. Eastwood then solidified his iconic status as Inspector "Dirty" Harry Callahan in five films beginning with Dirty Harry (1971). He transitioned into directing with Play Misty for Me (1971) and has since become one of cinema's most acclaimed directors, winning multiple Academy Awards for films like Unforgiven and Million Dollar Baby.
