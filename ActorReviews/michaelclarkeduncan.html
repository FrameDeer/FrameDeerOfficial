Michael Clarke Duncan

Date of Birth: December 10, 1957

Location of Birth: Chicago, Illinois, USA

Height: 6'5" (196 cm)

Michael Clarke Duncan was an American actor renowned for his towering physical presence, deep resonant voice, and gentle demeanor that often contrasted with his imposing stature. Best known for his Academy Award-nominated performance in The Green Mile, he brought remarkable sensitivity and emotional depth to his roles, often playing gentle giants with hidden wisdom and profound humanity.

Early Career

Michael Clarke Duncan began his career working various jobs in Chicago, including as a bodyguard for celebrities like Will Smith, Martin Lawrence, and Notorious B.I.G., before transitioning to acting. His early film roles included small parts in movies like Friday (1995) and Bulworth (1998). His breakthrough came with his powerful performance as John Coffey in Frank Darabont's The Green Mile (1999), for which he received an Academy Award nomination for Best Supporting Actor. This role showcased his ability to combine physical presence with emotional vulnerability, establishing him as a sought-after character actor. He went on to appear in major films like The Whole Nine Yards (2000), Planet of the Apes (2001), and The Scorpion King (2002), often bringing warmth and humanity to his characters despite his intimidating size.
