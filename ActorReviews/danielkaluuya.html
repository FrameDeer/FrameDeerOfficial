Daniel Kaluuya

Date of Birth: February 24, 1989

Location of Birth: London, England

Height: 5'9" (175 cm)

Daniel Kaluuya is an Academy Award-winning British actor, writer, and producer known for his powerful, emotionally resonant performances and remarkable versatility. Emerging from the UK's improv comedy scene, he has become one of the most respected actors of his generation, delivering critically acclaimed performances in both independent films and major studio productions that explore complex social themes.

Early Career

Daniel Kaluuya began his career as a teenager, writing for and performing in the Channel 4 teen drama Skins (2007-2009), where he also served as a contributing writer. He honed his skills through improvisational comedy with the group The Comedy Store Players and appeared in various British television series including Doctor Who and Black Mirror. His breakthrough film role came in 2017 with Jordan Peele's groundbreaking horror film Get Out, which earned him an Academy Award nomination for Best Actor. This was followed by his Oscar-winning performance as Fred Hampton in Judas and the Black Messiah (2021), cementing his status as one of the most compelling actors working today. His ability to portray both vulnerability and intensity has made him a sought-after talent in both dramatic and genre films.
