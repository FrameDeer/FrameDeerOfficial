Robert De Niro

Date of Birth: August 17, 1943

Location of Birth: Manhattan, New York City, U.S.

Height: 5'10" (178 cm)

Robert De Niro is widely regarded as one of the greatest actors in film history, known for his intense method acting and remarkable transformations for roles. With a career spanning over five decades, he has created some of cinema's most iconic characters through his legendary collaborations with director Martin Scorsese and his unparalleled commitment to character immersion.

Early Career

Robert De Niro began his acting career in the late 1960s, studying at the Stella Adler Conservatory and the Lee Strasberg Institute. He gained early recognition for his role in Bang the Drum Slowly (1973), but his breakthrough came with Martin Scorsese's Mean Streets (1973), beginning one of cinema's most celebrated director-actor partnerships. His career-defining performance came as the young Vito Corleone in The Godfather Part II (1974), for which he won his first Academy Award. Throughout the 1970s and 1980s, De Niro established himself as the premier method actor of his generation with transformative performances in Taxi Driver (1976), The Deer Hunter (1978), and Raging Bull (1980) - for which he won his second Oscar after famously gaining 60 pounds to portray boxer Jake LaMotta.

Major Awards

  • Academy Award for Best Actor – Raging Bull (1981)
  • Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor – The Godfather Part II (1975)
  • Golden Globe Award for Best Actor – Raging Bull (1981)
  • Golden Globe Cecil B. DeMille Award for Lifetime Achievement (2011)
  • BAFTA Award for Best Actor – Raging Bull (1981)
  • Screen Actors Guild Life Achievement Award (2020)
  • Kennedy Center Honors (2009)
  • Presidential Medal of Freedom (2016)
  • Film Society of Lincoln Center Gala Tribute (1997)
  • Venice Film Festival Golden Lion for Career Achievement (1993)
