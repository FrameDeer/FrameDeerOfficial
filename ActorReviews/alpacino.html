Al Pacino

Date of Birth: April 25, 1940

Location of Birth: New York City, New York, USA

Height: 5'7" (170 cm)

Al Pacino is an American actor and filmmaker considered one of the greatest and most influential actors of all time. Known for his intense, explosive acting style and iconic gangster roles, he has received numerous accolades including an Academy Award, two Tony Awards, and two Primetime Emmy Awards.

Early Career

Pacino began his career in theater, studying at the prestigious Actors Studio under Lee Strasberg. His breakthrough came with his Tony Award-winning performance in Does a Tiger Wear a Necktie? (1969). His film career exploded with his iconic portrayal of Michael Corleone in The Godfather (1972), which earned him his first Academy Award nomination and launched him into international stardom as part of the New Hollywood movement.

Major Awards

  • Academy Award for Best Actor – Scent of a Woman (1992)
  • Tony Award for Best Featured Actor – Does a Tiger Wear a Necktie? (1969)
  • Tony Award for Best Actor – The Basic Training of Pavlo Hummel (1977)
  • Primetime Emmy Award – Angels in America (2004)
  • Golden Globe Award for Best Actor – Scent of a Woman (1993)
  • AFI Life Achievement Award (2007)
  • National Medal of Arts (2011)
