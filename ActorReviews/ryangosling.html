Ryan Gosling

Date of Birth: November 12, 1980

Location of Birth: London, Ontario, Canada

Height: 6'0" (184 cm)

Ryan Gosling is a Canadian actor and musician known for his versatile performances in both independent films and major Hollywood productions. With his compelling screen presence and ability to portray complex characters, he has established himself as one of the most respected actors of his generation, earning critical acclaim and numerous awards throughout his career.

Early Career

Ryan Gosling began his career as a child actor on the Disney Channel's The Mickey Mouse Club (1993-1995) alongside future stars like Justin Timberlake and Britney Spears. He transitioned to more serious roles in the late 1990s, with his breakthrough coming in 2001's The Believer, where he played a Jewish neo-Nazi. His career-defining role came in 2004's The Notebook, which established him as a leading man and romantic heartthrob. Rather than capitalizing solely on his heartthrob status, Gosling deliberately chose challenging, diverse roles in films like Half Nelson, Lars and the Real Girl, and Blue Valentine, demonstrating his commitment to artistic integrity and complex character work.
