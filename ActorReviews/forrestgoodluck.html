Forrest Goodluck

Date of Birth: August 6, 1998

Location of Birth: Albuquerque, New Mexico, USA

Height: 5'8" (173 cm)

Forrest Goodluck is a rising Native American actor known for his powerful screen presence and commitment to authentic representation of Indigenous stories. A member of the Diné, Mandan, Hidatsa, and Tsimshian nations, he has quickly established himself as one of the most promising young Indigenous actors in Hollywood with his compelling performances in both independent films and major productions.

Early Career

Forrest Goodluck made his feature film debut with a breakthrough performance as Hawk, Leonardo DiCaprio's son in Alejandro G. Iñárritu's Academy Award-winning film The Revenant (2015). This high-profile debut at just 17 years old brought him immediate attention and established him as a talent to watch. He continued to build his career with roles that emphasize Indigenous representation and storytelling, including his performance in the independent film The Night Guard (2016). Goodluck has been selective about his projects, focusing on meaningful roles that contribute to the visibility of Native American stories in mainstream cinema while also pursuing his education and advocating for Indigenous rights and representation in the entertainment industry.
