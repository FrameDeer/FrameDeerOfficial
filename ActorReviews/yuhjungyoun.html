Yuh-Jung Youn

Date of Birth: June 19, 1947

Location of Birth: Kaesong, Gyeonggi Province, Korea

Height: 5'3" (160 cm)

Yuh-Jung Youn is an Academy Award-winning South Korean actress who has enjoyed a prolific career spanning over five decades. Known for her remarkable versatility, sharp wit, and authentic portrayals of complex characters, she became an international sensation with her Oscar-winning performance in Minari, making history as the first Korean actress to win an Academy Award.

Early Career

Yuh-Jung Youn began her acting career in 1966 after winning a talent contest hosted by the TBC television network. She quickly became one of South Korea's most prominent actresses, starring in numerous films and television dramas throughout the 1970s and 1980s. Her early notable works include Woman of Fire (1971) and Promising Woman (1974). After a period of reduced activity, she made a successful comeback in the 2000s with memorable roles in films like A Good Lawyer's Wife (2003) and The Housemaid (2010). Her international breakthrough came with her portrayal of the sharp-tongued yet loving grandmother Soon-ja in Lee Isaac Chung's Minari (2020), which earned her widespread critical acclaim and numerous awards, including the historic Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress.

Major Awards

  • Korean Association of Film Critics Award for Best Actress – Woman of Fire (1971)
  • Asian Film Award for Best Supporting Actress – The Housemaid (2011)
  • Buil Film Awards for Best Actress – Canola (2016)
