Nicole Kidman

Date of Birth: June 20, 1967

Location of Birth: Honolulu, Hawaii, U.S.

Height: 5'11" (180 cm)

Nicole Kidman is an Academy Award-winning actress known for her remarkable versatility, commanding screen presence, and fearless choice of complex roles. With a career spanning over three decades, she has established herself as one of the most respected and daring actresses in contemporary cinema, seamlessly transitioning between Hollywood blockbusters and challenging independent films.

Early Career

Nicole Kidman began her acting career in Australia during the 1980s, appearing in various Australian films and television shows. Her breakthrough came with the thriller Dead Calm (1989), which brought her international attention. She made her Hollywood debut opposite Tom Cruise in Days of Thunder (1990), beginning both a professional collaboration and personal relationship that would dominate media attention throughout the 1990s. During this period, she demonstrated her range in films like Billy Bathgate (1991), To Die For (1995) - which earned her a Golden Globe - and The Portrait of a Lady (1996). Her career-defining transformation came with Baz Luhrmann's Moulin Rouge! (2001), followed immediately by her Academy Award-winning performance in The Hours (2002), establishing her as a serious dramatic actress of the highest caliber.

Major Awards

  • Academy Award for Best Actress – The Hours (2003)
  • BAFTA Award for Best Actress – The Hours (2003)
  • Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – The Hours (2003)
  • Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – To Die For (1996)
  • Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress – Big Little Lies (2017)
  • Silver Bear for Best Actress – The Hours (2003)
  • AACTA Award for Best Actress – Lion (2016)
  • Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance – The Hours (2003)
  • Volpi Cup for Best Actress – The Portrait of a Lady (1996)
  • Order of Australia for services to the arts (2006)
