Jonah Hill

Date of Birth: December 20, 1983

Location of Birth: Los Angeles, California, USA

Height: 5'7" (170 cm)

Jonah Hill is a two-time Academy Award-nominated American actor, filmmaker, and director known for his remarkable evolution from comedic supporting roles to acclaimed dramatic performances. Beginning his career as a scene-stealing comedic actor, he has successfully transitioned to become one of Hollywood's most respected dramatic talents while also establishing himself as an accomplished writer and director.

Early Career

Jonah Hill began his career with small roles in films like I Heart Huckabees (2004) before his breakthrough comedic performance in Superbad (2007), which established him as a rising star in Hollywood comedy. He continued to build his reputation with successful comedies like Forgetting Sarah Marshall (2008) and Get Him to the Greek (2010). Hill's career took a dramatic turn with his Oscar-nominated performance in Moneyball (2011), showcasing his ability to handle serious dramatic material. He received a second Oscar nomination for The Wolf of Wall Street (2013), cementing his status as a versatile actor. More recently, Hill has expanded into writing and directing with his critically acclaimed debut Mid90s (2018) and the Netflix documentary series Stutz (2022), demonstrating his growth as a multifaceted filmmaker.
