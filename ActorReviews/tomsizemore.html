Tom Sizemore

Date of Birth: November 29, 1961

Location of Birth: Detroit, Michigan, USA

Height: 5'11" (180 cm)

Tom Sizemore was an American actor known for his intense, often volatile performances and his portrayals of tough, morally complex characters. Rising to prominence in the 1990s with memorable roles in major films, his career was marked by both critical acclaim and personal struggles that impacted his professional trajectory.

Early Career

Tom Sizemore began his acting career in the late 1980s after studying theater at Temple University and Wayne State University. His early work included small roles in films like Born on the Fourth of July (1989) and Lock Up (1989). His breakthrough came with his powerful performance as Detective Jack Scagnetti in Oliver Stone's Natural Born Killers (1994), which showcased his ability to portray intense, disturbed characters. This was followed by his critically acclaimed role as Sergeant Mike Horvath in Steven Spielberg's Saving Private Ryan (1998), which brought him widespread recognition. Throughout the late 1990s and early 2000s, he delivered memorable performances in films like Heat (1995), Black Hawk Down (2001), and Pearl Harbor (2001). However, his career was significantly affected by legal issues and substance abuse problems that limited his opportunities in later years.
