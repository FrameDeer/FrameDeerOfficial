Anya Taylor-Joy

Date of Birth: April 16, 1996

Location of Birth: Miami, Florida, USA

Height: 5'8" (173 cm)

Anya Taylor-Joy is a Golden Globe-winning actress known for her distinctive ethereal presence, captivating screen performances, and remarkable versatility across horror, period drama, and fantasy genres. With her striking features and ability to portray complex, often otherworldly characters, she has quickly established herself as one of the most compelling and sought-after actors of her generation.

Early Career

Anya Taylor-Joy began her acting career with a small role in the horror film The Vampire Academy (2014), but her breakthrough came with her stunning debut lead performance in Robert Eggers' The Witch (2015), which earned her widespread critical acclaim. She continued to build her reputation with roles in M. Night Shyamalan's Split (2016) and its sequel Glass (2019), as well as period pieces like Emma (2020). Her career reached new heights with her transformative performance as Beth Harmon in the Netflix miniseries The Queen's Gambit (2020), for which she won a Golden Globe and Screen Actors Guild Award. Since then, she has starred in major films like The Northman (2022) and The Menu (2022), solidifying her status as a premier dramatic actress.
