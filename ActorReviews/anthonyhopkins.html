Anthony Hopkins

Date of Birth: December 31, 1937

Location of Birth: Margam, Port Talbot, Wales

Height: 5'8" (173 cm)

Sir Anthony Hopkins is one of the most acclaimed and respected actors of his generation, known for his commanding screen presence, remarkable versatility, and intense character portrayals. With a career spanning over six decades, he has created some of cinema's most memorable characters and continues to deliver powerful performances well into his eighties.

Early Career

Anthony Hopkins began his acting career on the British stage after studying at the Royal Welsh College of Music & Drama and the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art. He gained recognition in the 1960s with the Royal National Theatre, where he served as understudy to Laurence Olivier. His film career began with The Lion in Winter (1968), but his breakthrough came with his chilling portrayal of Dr. Hannibal Lecter in The Silence of the Lambs (1991), for which he won his first Academy Award. Despite only 16 minutes of screen time, his performance became one of the most iconic in cinema history. Throughout the 1990s and beyond, Hopkins demonstrated his remarkable range in films like The Remains of the Day (1993), Nixon (1995), and Amistad (1997), establishing himself as one of Britain's most distinguished actors.
