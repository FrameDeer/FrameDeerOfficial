John David Washington

Date of Birth: July 28, 1984

Location of Birth: Los Angeles, California, USA

Height: 5'9" (175 cm)

John David Washington is an American actor and former professional football player who has emerged as one of Hollywood's most compelling leading men. Known for his intense physicality, emotional depth, and charismatic screen presence, he has successfully carved his own path separate from his famous father Denzel Washington, establishing himself as a formidable talent through critically acclaimed performances in both independent films and major studio productions.

Early Career

John David Washington initially pursued a career in professional football, playing as a running back for the St. Louis Rams (now Los Angeles Rams) in the NFL and later for the Sacramento Mountain Lions in the United Football League. His acting career began with small roles in his father's films, including a brief appearance in Malcolm X (1992) as a child. However, his serious commitment to acting began after his football career ended due to injury. His breakthrough came with the HBO comedy series Ballers (2015-2019), where he played Ricky Jerret for five seasons. His film career skyrocketed with his powerful leading performance as Ron Stallworth in Spike Lee's BlacKkKlansman (2018), which earned him a Golden Globe nomination and critical acclaim. This was followed by starring roles in Christopher Nolan's Tenet (2020) and other high-profile projects, cementing his status as a leading man.
