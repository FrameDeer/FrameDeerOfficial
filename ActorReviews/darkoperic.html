Darko Perić

Date of Birth: November 13, 1977

Location of Birth: Zrenjanin, Serbia

Height: 6'4" (193 cm)

Darko Perić is a Serbian actor who gained international recognition for his role as Mirko Dragić (Helsinki) in the global Netflix phenomenon Money Heist (La Casa de Papel). Known for his imposing physical presence, gentle demeanor, and memorable dance scenes, he has become one of the most beloved characters in the series and a fan favorite worldwide, showcasing the perfect balance between toughness and tenderness.

Early Career

Darko Perić began his acting career in Serbian theater and film after studying acting at the Faculty of Dramatic Arts in Belgrade. He built his career through various Serbian television series, films, and theater productions, establishing himself as a respected actor in the Balkan region. His international breakthrough came with his portrayal of Helsinki in Money Heist (2017-2021), where his character's gentle giant persona and unexpected dance moves captured audiences' hearts worldwide. Despite his intimidating appearance, Perić's performance showcased Helsinki's emotional depth, loyalty, and surprising tenderness, particularly in his relationship with Nairobi. His character became known for memorable moments that balanced action with emotional vulnerability, including his iconic dance scenes and his touching friendship with other crew members. Perić's ability to bring complexity and humanity to his character established him as an international star and demonstrated his remarkable acting range.
