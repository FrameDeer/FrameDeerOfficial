Tilda Swinton

Date of Birth: November 5, 1960

Location of Birth: London, England

Height: 5'11" (180 cm)

Tilda Swinton is an Academy Award-winning Scottish actress known for her androgynous appearance, transformative performances, and fearless artistic choices. Renowned for her collaborations with visionary directors and her ability to completely inhabit diverse characters, she has established herself as one of cinema's most distinctive and respected artists.

Early Career

Tilda Swinton began her career in the 1980s after graduating from Cambridge University, initially working with the Royal Shakespeare Company. She gained critical recognition through her extensive collaborations with director Derek Jarman in films like Caravaggio (1986) and The Last of England (1987). Her breakthrough to international audiences came with her mesmerizing performance as Orlando in Sally Potter's 1992 adaptation of Virginia Woolf's novel. Throughout the 1990s, she continued to work in independent and arthouse cinema, building a reputation for her unconventional choices and chameleonic abilities before achieving mainstream recognition in the 2000s with roles in films like Michael Clayton, for which she won an Academy Award.

Major Awards

  • Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress – Michael Clayton (2008)
  • BAFTA Award for Best Actress – We Need to Talk About Kevin (2012)
  • European Film Award for Best Actress – Julia (2009)
  • Volpi Cup for Best Actress – I Am Love (2009)
  • Los Angeles Film Critics Association Award for Best Actress – We Need to Talk About Kevin (2011)
  • New York Film Critics Circle Award for Best Supporting Actress – Michael Clayton (2007)
  • Saturn Award Nomination for Best Supporting Actress – Snowpiercer (2014)
  • Golden Globe Nomination for Best Supporting Actress – Michael Clayton (2008)
  • Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance – Michael Clayton (2008)
  • Honorary Doctorate from the Royal Scottish Academy of Music and Drama (2006)
