Willem Dafoe

Date of Birth: July 22, 1955

Location of Birth: Appleton, Wisconsin, USA

Height: 5'9" (175 cm)

Willem Dafoe is a four-time Academy Award-nominated American actor renowned for his intense screen presence, distinctive features, and remarkable versatility across a wide range of characters. Known for his commitment to both mainstream and independent cinema, Dafoe has built a career spanning over four decades, establishing himself as one of Hollywood's most respected and fearless character actors.

Early Career

Willem Dafoe began his career in experimental theater, co-founding The Wooster Group in New York City, where he performed and developed his craft for several years. His film debut came in Michael Cimino's controversial epic Heaven's Gate (1980), though most of his scenes were cut. His breakthrough role came as Sergeant Elias in Oliver Stone's Platoon (1986), which earned him his first Academy Award nomination for Best Supporting Actor. Throughout the 1980s and 1990s, Dafoe built a reputation for playing complex, often villainous characters in films like The Last Temptation of Christ (1988), Mississippi Burning (1988), and Shadow of the Vampire (2000), while also maintaining his commitment to experimental theater and independent cinema.
