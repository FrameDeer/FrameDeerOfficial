Domhnall Gleeson

Date of Birth: May 12, 1983

Location of Birth: Dublin, Ireland

Height: 6'1" (185 cm)

Domhnall Gleeson is an Irish actor and writer known for his remarkable versatility, intellectual presence, and ability to seamlessly transition between independent films and major blockbuster franchises. The son of acclaimed actor Brendan Gleeson, he has established himself as one of Ireland's most respected acting talents with a diverse range of compelling performances.

Early Career

Domhnall Gleeson began his career in Irish theatre and made his film debut in his father's film Studs (2006). His breakthrough came with his role as Bill Weasley in Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows (2010-2011), which introduced him to international audiences. He gained critical acclaim for his performance in Never Let Me Go (2010) and particularly for his starring role in About Time (2013), which showcased his romantic lead capabilities. Gleeson's career expanded significantly with his collaborations in multiple Alex Garland projects including Ex Machina (2014) and Annihilation (2018), as well as his roles in major franchises like Star Wars as General Hux. His ability to balance mainstream success with critically acclaimed independent work has made him one of the most interesting actors of his generation.
