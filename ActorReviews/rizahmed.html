Riz Ahmed

Date of Birth: December 1, 1982

Location of Birth: London, England

Height: 5'8" (173 cm)

Riz Ahmed is an Academy Award-winning British actor, rapper, and activist known for his powerful, nuanced performances and commitment to diverse storytelling. As one of the most prominent British-Pakistani artists working today, he has broken barriers in the entertainment industry while maintaining a successful parallel career as a musician and using his platform to advocate for representation and social justice.

Early Career

Riz Ahmed began his career with a series of short films and television appearances in the mid-2000s after studying philosophy, politics, and economics at Oxford University and attending the Central School of Speech and Drama. His breakthrough came with his powerful performance as a Pakistani student radicalized by anti-terror laws in The Road to Guantanamo (2006). He gained wider recognition for his role in Michael Winterbottom's The Road to Guantanamo and continued to build his reputation with compelling performances in British independent films. His international breakthrough came with Nightcrawler (2014), followed by his Emmy-winning role in HBO's The Night Of (2016). He made history as the first Muslim and first Asian to win an acting Oscar for his performance in Sound of Metal (2020), cementing his status as one of Britain's most important contemporary actors.
