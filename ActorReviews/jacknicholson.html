Jack Nicholson

Date of Birth: April 22, 1937

Location of Birth: New York City, New York, USA

Height: 5'9" (175 cm)

Jack Nicholson is an American retired actor and filmmaker widely regarded as one of the greatest actors in Hollywood history. Known for his iconic grin, rebellious persona, and versatile performances spanning five decades, he has been nominated for twelve Academy Awards and won three, making him the most nominated male actor in Oscar history.

Early Career

Nicholson began his career in the 1950s working for animation legend William Hanna at MGM. His breakthrough came with his supporting role in Easy Rider (1969), which earned him his first Academy Award nomination. This was followed by his star-making performance in Five Easy Pieces (1970), establishing him as a leading figure of the New Hollywood era. His portrayal of complex, often anti-establishment characters made him one of the most distinctive and celebrated actors of his generation.

Major Awards

  • Academy Award for Best Actor – One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest (1975)
  • Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor – Terms of Endearment (1983)
  • Academy Award for Best Actor – As Good as It Gets (1997)
  • Golden Globe Award for Best Actor – One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest (1975)
  • Golden Globe Award for Best Actor – Chinatown (1974)
  • Golden Globe Award for Best Actor – Prizzi's Honor (1985)
  • BAFTA Award for Best Actor – One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest (1975)
  • AFI Life Achievement Award (1994)
  • Kennedy Center Honors (2001)
