Russell Crowe

Date of Birth: April 7, 1964

Location of Birth: Wellington, New Zealand

Height: 5'11" (182 cm)

Russell Crowe is an Academy Award-winning actor known for his intense method acting, powerful screen presence, and transformative performances. With a career spanning from Australian television to Hollywood stardom, he has established himself as one of the most formidable and respected actors of his generation, particularly renowned for his work in historical epics and dramatic roles.

Early Career

Russell Crowe began his acting career in Australia during the late 1980s, appearing in various Australian television series and films. His breakthrough came with the 1992 film Romper Stomper, where his intense portrayal of a neo-Nazi skinhead garnered critical acclaim and established his reputation as a serious actor. He gained international recognition with The Quick and the Dead (1995) and L.A. Confidential (1997), the latter earning him widespread praise for his performance as Detective Wendell "Bud" White. His career reached its zenith with back-to-back acclaimed performances in The Insider (1999), which earned him his first Academy Award nomination, and Gladiator (2000), for which he won the Oscar for Best Actor, cementing his status as a leading man in Hollywood.

Major Awards

  • BAFTA Award for Best Actor – Gladiator (2001)

  • Academy Award nomination for Best Actor – A Beautiful Mind (2002)
