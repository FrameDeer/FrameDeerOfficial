Meryl Streep

Date of Birth: June 22, 1949

Location of Birth: Summit, New Jersey, U.S.

Height: 5'6" (168 cm)

Meryl Streep is widely regarded as one of the greatest actresses in film history, holding the record for most Academy Award nominations of any actor. Known for her incredible versatility and mastery of accents, she has delivered iconic performances across every genre for over five decades, consistently demonstrating remarkable range and depth in her character portrayals.

Early Career

Meryl Streep began her career on the New York stage after graduating from Yale School of Drama. She made her Broadway debut in Trelawny of the Wells (1975) and quickly gained recognition for her theatrical work. Her film breakthrough came with The Deer Hunter (1978), earning her first Academy Award nomination. She won her first Oscar the following year for Kramer vs. Kramer (1979). Throughout the 1980s, Streep established herself as a formidable dramatic actress with a series of acclaimed performances in The French Lieutenant's Woman (1981), Sophie's Choice (1982) - for which she won her second Academy Award - and Out of Africa (1985), demonstrating an unprecedented ability to transform completely into each character.

Major Awards

  • Academy Award for Best Actress – Sophie's Choice (1983)
  • Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress – Kramer vs. Kramer (1980)
  • Record 21 Academy Award nominations (most of any actor)
  • Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – The Iron Lady (2012)
  • Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actress – Kramer vs. Kramer (1980)
  • BAFTA Award for Best Actress – The Iron Lady (2012)
  • Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress – Angels in America (2004)
  • Cecil B. DeMille Award for Lifetime Achievement (2017)
  • Kennedy Center Honors (2011)
  • Presidential Medal of Freedom (2014)
