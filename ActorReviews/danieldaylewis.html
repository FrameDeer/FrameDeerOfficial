Daniel Day-Lewis

Date of Birth: April 29, 1957

Location of Birth: London, England

Height: 6'1" (187 cm)

Sir Daniel Day-Lewis is a retired English actor renowned for his intense method acting style and remarkable transformations for roles. He is the only male actor to have won three Academy Awards for Best Actor and is widely regarded as one of the greatest actors in cinematic history.

Early Career

Day-Lewis began his career on stage at the Bristol Old Vic Theatre School and with the Royal Shakespeare Company. His film breakthrough came with My Beautiful Laundrette (1985) and A Room with a View (1985), showcasing his remarkable range. His first major international recognition came with his Oscar-nominated performance as Christy Brown in My Left Foot (1989), for which he won his first Academy Award, establishing his reputation for complete immersion in his characters.

Major Awards

  • Academy Award for Best Actor – My Left Foot (1989)
  • Academy Award for Best Actor – There Will Be Blood (2007)
  • Academy Award for Best Actor – Lincoln (2012)
  • BAFTA Award for Best Actor – My Left Foot (1989)
  • BAFTA Award for Best Actor – There Will Be Blood (2007)
  • Golden Globe Award for Best Actor – Gangs of New York (2002)
  • Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance – Lincoln (2012)
