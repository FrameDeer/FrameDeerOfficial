Sigourney Weaver

Date of Birth: October 8, 1949

Location of Birth: New York City, New York, USA

Height: 5'11" (180 cm)

Sigourney Weaver is an Academy Award-nominated American actress renowned for her powerful screen presence, versatility, and groundbreaking roles in science fiction. Known for her intelligent, strong female characters and commanding performances, she has become one of Hollywood's most respected and enduring actresses, particularly celebrated for her iconic role as Ellen Ripley in the Alien franchise.

Early Career

Sigourney Weaver began her career in theater after graduating from the Yale School of Drama. She made her film debut in a small role in Woody Allen's Annie Hall (1977), but her breakthrough came with Ridley Scott's science fiction horror film Alien (1979), where she created the iconic character of Ellen Ripley. This role revolutionized the portrayal of women in action and science fiction films, establishing Weaver as a major star. She continued to demonstrate her versatility with acclaimed performances in The Year of Living Dangerously (1982) and received her first Academy Award nomination for James Cameron's Aliens (1986). Weaver further showcased her range with comedic roles in Ghostbusters (1984) and dramatic performances in Gorillas in the Mist (1988) and Working Girl (1988), becoming the first person to receive two Oscar nominations in the same year for two different films.
