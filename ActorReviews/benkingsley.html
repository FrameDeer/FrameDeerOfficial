Ben Kingsley

Date of Birth: December 31, 1943

Location of Birth: Snainton, North Riding of Yorkshire, England

Height: 5'9" (175 cm)

Sir Ben Kingsley is an Academy Award-winning English actor renowned for his remarkable versatility, chameleonic transformations, and commanding screen presence. With a career spanning over five decades, he has established himself as one of the most respected and accomplished actors of his generation, known for his ability to completely inhabit diverse historical and fictional characters across multiple genres.

Early Career

Ben Kingsley began his acting career on stage with the Royal Shakespeare Company in the late 1960s, building a strong reputation in classical theatre. His film debut came in 1972 with Fear Is the Key, but his international breakthrough arrived with his iconic portrayal of Mahatma Gandhi in Richard Attenborough's epic Gandhi (1982), for which he won the Academy Award for Best Actor. This transformative performance established him as a major international star capable of profound character immersion. Throughout the 1980s and 1990s, he continued to deliver acclaimed performances in films like Bugsy (1991) and Schindler's List (1993), while also exploring more commercial projects. His career has been marked by an extraordinary range, from historical figures to complex villains, demonstrating his unparalleled ability to disappear into any role he undertakes.
