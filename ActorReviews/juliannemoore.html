Julianne Moore

Date of Birth: December 3, 1960

Location of Birth: Fort Bragg, North Carolina, USA

Height: 5'4" (163 cm)

Julianne Moore is an Academy Award-winning American actress and author renowned for her nuanced portrayals of complex, emotionally vulnerable characters. With her distinctive red hair and remarkable versatility, she has delivered critically acclaimed performances across independent films and major studio productions, establishing herself as one of the most respected actresses of her generation.

Early Career

Julianne Moore began her acting career in the early 1980s after graduating from Boston University's School of the Arts. She started with theater work and television soap operas, notably playing dual roles on As the World Turns from 1985 to 1988, which earned her a Daytime Emmy Award. Her breakthrough in film came with Robert Altman's Short Cuts (1993), followed by memorable supporting roles in The Fugitive (1993) and Nine Months (1995). However, it was her powerful performances in Todd Haynes' Safe (1995) and Paul Thomas Anderson's Boogie Nights (1997) that established her as a serious dramatic actress capable of tackling challenging, complex characters with remarkable depth and authenticity.
