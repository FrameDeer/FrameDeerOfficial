Benedict Cumberbatch

Date of Birth: July 19, 1976

Location of Birth: London, England

Height: 6'0" (183 cm)

Benedict Cumberbatch is an Academy Award-nominated English actor renowned for his distinctive voice, intellectual presence, and remarkable versatility. Known for his portrayals of complex, intelligent characters, he has achieved international fame through both blockbuster franchises and critically acclaimed independent films, establishing himself as one of Britain's most respected actors.

Early Career

Benedict Cumberbatch began his acting career in theatre after graduating from the London Academy of Music and Dramatic Art. He built his reputation through stage work and television appearances in the early 2000s, with notable roles in Hawking (2004) as Stephen Hawking and To the Ends of the Earth (2005). His breakthrough came in 2010 when he was cast as Sherlock Holmes in the BBC series Sherlock, which earned him international recognition and critical acclaim. This role catapulted him to global stardom and led to major film opportunities, including his Oscar-nominated performance as Alan Turing in The Imitation Game (2014) and his entry into the Marvel Cinematic Universe as Doctor Strange.
