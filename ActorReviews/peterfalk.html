Peter Falk

Date of Birth: September 16, 1927

Date of Death: June 23, 2011

Location of Birth: New York City, New York, USA

Height: 5'6" (168 cm)

Peter Falk was an American actor, best known for his iconic role as Lieutenant Columbo in the long-running television series Columbo. With his distinctive appearance, including a glass eye and rumpled raincoat, Falk created one of television's most beloved and enduring detective characters. His career spanned over five decades, earning him numerous awards and critical acclaim for both television and film work.

Early Career

Peter Falk began his acting career in the late 1950s after studying at The New School in New York. He made his Broadway debut in "Diary of a Scoundrel" (1956) and quickly transitioned to film, earning Academy Award nominations for Best Supporting Actor for Murder, Inc. (1960) and Pocketful of Miracles (1961). His breakthrough television role came in 1968 with the television movie "Prescription: Murder," which introduced Lieutenant Columbo. The character became so popular that it spawned the long-running series Columbo (1971-2003), making Falk a household name and earning him four Primetime Emmy Awards. Despite his television success, Falk continued to work in film, delivering memorable performances in classics like It's a Mad, Mad, Mad, Mad World (1963) and The Great Race (1965), and later in acclaimed films like The Princess Bride (1987) and Wings of Desire (1987).
