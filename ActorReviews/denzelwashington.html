Denzel Washington

Date of Birth: December 28, 1954

Location of Birth: Mount Vernon, New York, USA

Height: 6'1" (185 cm)

Denzel Washington is an Academy Award-winning actor, director, and producer widely regarded as one of the greatest actors of his generation. Known for his powerful screen presence, commanding performances, and remarkable versatility, he has maintained an illustrious career spanning over four decades while consistently delivering critically acclaimed work across diverse genres.

Early Career

Denzel Washington began his acting career after graduating from Fordham University and studying at the American Conservatory Theater. His professional breakthrough came with the television medical drama St. Elsewhere (1982-1988), where he played Dr. Philip Chandler for six seasons. His film career started with supporting roles in movies like Carbon Copy (1981) and A Soldier's Story (1984). His major breakthrough came with his Oscar-winning performance in Glory (1989), which established him as a serious dramatic actor. This was followed by his iconic role as Malcolm X in Spike Lee's 1992 biopic, cementing his status as one of Hollywood's most respected and bankable leading men.
