Edward Burns

Date of Birth: January 29, 1968

Location of Birth: Woodside, Queens, New York, USA

Height: 6'0" (183 cm)

Edward Burns is an American actor, filmmaker, and producer known for his work in independent cinema and his distinctive New York-centric storytelling. As a pioneering figure in the 1990s independent film movement, he gained recognition for writing, directing, and starring in low-budget films that captured authentic American experiences, particularly working-class Irish-American life.

Early Career

Edward Burns began his career while working as a production assistant for Entertainment Tonight. His breakthrough came with his self-financed debut feature The Brothers McMullen (1995), which he wrote, directed, produced, and starred in. The film won the Grand Jury Prize at the Sundance Film Festival and established Burns as a major new voice in independent cinema. He followed this success with She's the One (1996) and continued to build his reputation as both an actor and filmmaker. While maintaining his independent film roots, Burns also appeared in major studio productions like Saving Private Ryan (1998) and developed television projects. Throughout his career, he has remained committed to telling personal, character-driven stories often set in his native New York.
