Rob Reiner

Date of Birth: March 6, 1947

Location of Birth: Bronx, New York, USA

Height: 6'2" (188 cm)

Rob Reiner is an American actor, director, producer, and activist who has enjoyed a remarkable career spanning over five decades. Known for his incredible versatility, he first gained fame as an actor on the groundbreaking television series All in the Family before transitioning into one of Hollywood's most successful and respected directors, helming numerous classic films across multiple genres.

Early Career

Rob Reiner began his career as an actor in the late 1960s, appearing in various television shows before landing his breakthrough role as Michael "Meathead" Stivic on the iconic sitcom All in the Family (1971-1978). The role earned him two Emmy Awards and established him as a prominent television actor. In the late 1970s, he transitioned into directing with the rock documentary This Is Spinal Tap (1984), which he also co-wrote and appeared in. His directorial career skyrocketed with a string of critically and commercially successful films including Stand by Me (1986), The Princess Bride (1987), When Harry Met Sally... (1989), and A Few Good Men (1992). Throughout his career, Reiner has continued to act while maintaining his successful directing career, establishing himself as one of Hollywood's most reliable and talented filmmakers.

Major Awards

  • Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actor – All in the Family (1974, 1978)
  • Golden Globe Award for Best Actor – All in the Family (1975)
  • Directors Guild of America Award Nomination – A Few Good Men (1992)
  • Writers Guild of America Award Nomination – This Is Spinal Tap (1984)
  • Humanitas Prize – Stand by Me (1986)
  • American Film Institute Lifetime Achievement Award (2017)
  • Hollywood Walk of Fame Star (1991)
  • Critics' Choice Movie Award Nomination – The American President (1995)
  • Saturn Award Nomination – Misery (1990)
  • Political Activism Awards – Various Civil Rights Work
