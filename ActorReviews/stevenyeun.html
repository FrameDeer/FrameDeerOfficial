Steven Yeun

Date of Birth: December 21, 1983

Location of Birth: Seoul, South Korea

Height: 5'9" (175 cm)

Steven Yeun is a history-making Academy Award-nominated American actor known for his subtle emotional depth, remarkable versatility, and groundbreaking achievements in Hollywood. As the first Asian-American actor to receive an Oscar nomination for Best Actor, he has established himself as one of the most respected and compelling performers of his generation, equally adept at television drama, independent film, and voice acting.

Early Career

Steven Yeun began his career with small television roles after moving to Chicago and performing with the Second City comedy troupe. His breakthrough came with his role as Glenn Rhee in the hit AMC series The Walking Dead (2010-2016), which brought him international recognition and made him one of the most beloved characters on television. Following his departure from the series, Yeun deliberately chose challenging, artistically ambitious projects that showcased his range, including Bong Joon-ho's Okja (2017) and Lee Chang-dong's Burning (2018). His career reached historic heights with his Academy Award-nominated performance in Minari (2020), making him the first Asian-American nominee for Best Actor. He has since continued to choose innovative projects across film and television, including his acclaimed work in Beef (2023).

Major Awards

  • Academy Award Nomination for Best Actor – Minari (2021) - First Asian-American nominee in this category
  • Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actor – Beef (2023)
  • Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance – Beef (2024)
  • Critics' Choice Television Award for Best Actor – Beef (2023)
  • Gotham Independent Film Award for Breakthrough Actor – Minari (2020)
  • Independent Spirit Award for Best Male Lead – Minari (2021)
  • Saturn Award Nomination for Best Supporting Actor – The Walking Dead (2012, 2013)
  • BAFTA Nomination for Best Actor – Minari (2021)
  • Golden Globe Nomination for Best Actor – Minari (2021)
  • MTV Movie Award Nomination for Best Scared-As-S**t Performance – The Walking Dead (2015)
