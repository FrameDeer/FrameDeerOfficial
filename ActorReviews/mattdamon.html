Matt Damon

Date of Birth: October 8, 1970

Location of Birth: Cambridge, Massachusetts, U.S.

Height: 5'10" (178 cm)

Matt Damon is an Academy Award-winning actor, screenwriter, and producer known for his versatile performances in both dramatic and action roles. With a career spanning over three decades, he has established himself as one of Hollywood's most reliable and respected actors, equally adept at blockbuster franchises and intimate character studies.

Early Career

Matt Damon began his acting career while studying at Harvard University, making his film debut in Mystic Pizza (1988). His breakthrough came with Good Will Hunting (1997), which he co-wrote with childhood friend Ben Affleck. The film earned them an Academy Award for Best Original Screenplay and established Damon as a serious actor. He followed this success with critically acclaimed performances in Saving Private Ryan (1998) and The Talented Mr. Ripley (1999), demonstrating his range from heroic everyman to complex antagonist. Throughout the 2000s, Damon successfully balanced commercial success with the Bourne franchise while continuing to work with esteemed directors like Steven Soderbergh and Martin Scorsese, cementing his status as one of Hollywood's most versatile leading men.

Major Awards

  • Academy Award for Best Original Screenplay – Good Will Hunting (1998)
  • Golden Globe Award for Best Screenplay – Good Will Hunting (1998)
  • Academy Award nomination for Best Actor – Good Will Hunting (1998)
  • Golden Globe Award for Best Actor – The Martian (2016)
  • Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Drama Series – Project Greenlight (2005)
  • Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Cast – Good Will Hunting (1998)
  • National Board of Review Award for Best Cast – Invictus (2009)
  • Critics' Choice Movie Award for Best Acting Ensemble – Good Will Hunting (1998)
  • MTV Movie Award for Best Fight – The Bourne Ultimatum (2008)
  • Harvard Arts Medal (2013)
