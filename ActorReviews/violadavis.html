Viola Davis

Date of Birth: August 11, 1965

Location of Birth: St. Matthews, South Carolina, USA

Height: 5'5" (165 cm)

Viola Davis is an Academy Award-winning actress and producer, widely regarded as one of the greatest actresses of her generation. She is the first Black actress to achieve the "Triple Crown of Acting"—winning an Academy Award, Emmy Award, and Tony Award—and is celebrated for her powerful, emotionally resonant performances across stage, television, and film.

Early Career

Viola Davis began her career in theater after graduating from the Juilliard School in 1993. She made her Broadway debut in 1996's Seven Guitars, earning her first Tony Award nomination. Throughout the late 1990s and early 2000s, Davis built a reputation as a formidable stage actress while taking supporting roles in film and television. Her breakthrough film performance came in 2008's Doubt, where her 8-minute scene with Meryl Streep earned her an Academy Award nomination for Best Supporting Actress and widespread critical acclaim. This performance marked her arrival as a major dramatic force in Hollywood and paved the way for her subsequent leading roles.
