Barry Pepper

Date of Birth: April 4, 1970

Location of Birth: Campbell River, British Columbia, Canada

Height: 5'10" (178 cm)

Barry Pepper is a Canadian actor known for his intense character work, remarkable versatility, and ability to completely transform for his roles. With a career spanning over two decades, he has established himself as one of Hollywood's most reliable and compelling character actors, equally adept at playing heroic figures, complex villains, and everything in between.

Early Career

Barry Pepper began his acting career in Canadian television productions in the early 1990s before making his Hollywood breakthrough. His first major role came in the miniseries The Lone Gunmen (1994), but it was his performance as the devout sniper Private Daniel Jackson in Saving Private Ryan (1998) that brought him international recognition. Pepper continued to build his reputation with memorable roles in films like The Green Mile (1999) and Enemy of the State (1998). His career reached new heights with his critically acclaimed portrayal of Roger Maris in the television film 61* (2001), which earned him an Emmy nomination. Since then, he has continued to deliver powerful performances in both film and television, showcasing his range across various genres.
