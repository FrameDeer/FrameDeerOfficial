Michael B. Jordan

Date of Birth: February 9, 1987

Location of Birth: Santa Ana, California, USA

Height: 6'0" (183 cm)

Michael B. Jordan is an acclaimed American actor and producer who has emerged as one of Hollywood's most prominent leading men. Known for his charismatic screen presence, physical intensity, and emotional depth, he gained widespread recognition through his collaborations with director Ryan Coogler and has since become a major box office draw and cultural icon.

Early Career

Michael B. Jordan began his acting career as a child, appearing in various modeling gigs and commercials before landing his first television roles in the late 1990s. His early breakthrough came with the role of Wallace in the critically acclaimed HBO series The Wire (2002), followed by a starring role as quarterback Vince Howard on Friday Night Lights (2009-2011). However, his career-defining moment arrived with his powerful performance as Oscar Grant in Ryan Coogler's Fruitvale Station (2013), which earned him widespread critical acclaim. This was followed by his role as Adonis Johnson in the Rocky spin-off Creed (2015), which solidified his status as a leading man and his portrayal of Erik Killmonger in Black Panther (2018) made him one of Marvel's most memorable villains.
