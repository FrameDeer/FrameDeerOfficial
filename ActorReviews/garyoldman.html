Gary Oldman

Date of Birth: March 21, 1958

Location of Birth: London, England

Height: 5'9" (175 cm)

Gary Oldman is an Academy Award-winning English actor and filmmaker renowned for his intense method acting and remarkable versatility. Often described as one of the greatest actors of his generation, he is known for his complete physical and emotional transformations into diverse characters, from historical figures to complex villains.

Early Career

Gary Oldman began his career on the British stage after studying at the Rose Bruford College of Speech and Drama. His breakthrough film role came in 1986's Sid and Nancy, where his transformative performance as Sex Pistols bassist Sid Vicious earned critical acclaim. He quickly established himself as a formidable character actor with roles in Prick Up Your Ears (1987) as Joe Orton and State of Grace (1990). His move to Hollywood in the early 1990s saw him take on iconic villain roles including Lee Harvey Oswald in JFK (1991), Dracula in Bram Stoker's Dracula (1992), and the corrupt DEA agent in Leon: The Professional (1994), cementing his reputation as a master of character transformation.
