Ethan Hawke

Date of Birth: November 6, 1970

Location of Birth: Austin, Texas, USA

Height: 5'10" (178 cm)

Ethan Hawke is a four-time Academy Award-nominated American actor, writer, and director known for his intellectual depth, artistic versatility, and commitment to independent cinema. With a career spanning over four decades, he has established himself as one of Hollywood's most respected and thoughtful artists, equally adept at blockbuster films and intimate character studies.

Early Career

Ethan Hawke made his film debut at age 14 in the sci-fi adventure Explorers (1985), but his breakthrough came with Peter Weir's Dead Poets Society (1989), where his performance as the sensitive prep school student Todd Anderson garnered critical attention. Throughout the 1990s, Hawke built his reputation in independent films like Reality Bites (1994) and Before Sunrise (1995), establishing himself as a prominent voice of Generation X. His collaboration with director Richard Linklater on the "Before" trilogy and other projects, along with his acclaimed performance in Training Day (2001), solidified his status as a serious actor capable of both mainstream success and artistic integrity.

Major Awards

  • Academy Award Nomination for Best Supporting Actor – Training Day (2002)
  • Academy Award Nomination for Best Adapted Screenplay – Before Sunset (2005)
  • Academy Award Nomination for Best Adapted Screenplay – Before Midnight (2014)
  • Academy Award Nomination for Best Supporting Actor – Boyhood (2015)
  • Tony Award Nomination for Best Featured Actor – The Coast of Utopia (2007)
  • Tony Award Nomination for Best Actor – Macbeth (2022)
  • Critics' Choice Movie Award for Best Supporting Actor – Boyhood (2015)
  • Gotham Independent Film Award for Best Actor – First Reformed (2018)
  • Saturn Award Nomination for Best Actor – The Black Phone (2022)
  • Independent Spirit Award Nomination for Best Male Lead – First Reformed (2018)
