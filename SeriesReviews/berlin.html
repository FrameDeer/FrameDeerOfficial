Berlin (2023)

Genre: Crime, Drama, Thriller | Seasons: 1 | Network: Netflix

A Money Heist prequel series focusing on the charismatic and unpredictable Berlin during his golden years before the events of the original series. Set in Paris, Berlin and his new crew attempt one of his most ambitious heists ever - stealing €44 million worth of jewels in a single afternoon.

FrameDeer Thoughts

Berlin successfully expands the Money Heist universe by focusing on one of its most compelling and morally complex characters. Pedro Alonso reprises his role as the charming, narcissistic, and unpredictable master thief, delivering a performance that both honors his original portrayal and explores new dimensions of the character.

The series wisely shifts from the political undertones of the original Money Heist to a more personal, character-driven narrative. Set during Berlin's "golden years" before his terminal illness diagnosis, the show captures him at his peak - brilliant, arrogant, and utterly captivating. The Paris setting provides a fresh backdrop that contrasts beautifully with the Spanish locations of the original series.

The new ensemble cast brings fresh energy while maintaining the chemistry that made Money Heist so compelling. Michelle Jenner's Keila, a brilliant cybersecurity expert, and Begoña Vargas's Cameron, a skilled locksmith, stand out as particularly strong additions to the heist crew. Their dynamic with Berlin creates the perfect balance of tension and camaraderie.

While the series maintains the signature Money Heist elements - intricate planning, unexpected twists, and high-stakes tension - it also carves its own identity with a more romantic, almost whimsical tone. Berlin's love story with Camille (Samantha Siqueiros) adds emotional depth to his character, revealing vulnerabilities that were only hinted at in the original series.

The heist itself is brilliantly conceived, focusing on stealing €44 million worth of jewels from an auction house in Paris. The planning sequences showcase Berlin's genius while allowing each crew member to demonstrate their unique skills. The series successfully balances the technical aspects of the heist with the emotional journeys of its characters.

Final verdict: A worthy addition to the Money Heist universe that deepens our understanding of one of its most fascinating characters. While it may not reach the revolutionary heights of the original, Berlin delivers an entertaining, stylish, and emotionally resonant heist drama that will satisfy both longtime fans and newcomers.

The Making of Berlin

Creating a prequel series centered on Berlin presented both opportunities and challenges for creator Álex Pina and his team. The goal was to explore the character's golden years while maintaining the essence that made him so compelling in Money Heist. The decision to set the series in Paris allowed for a fresh visual palette while maintaining the European sophistication that defines Berlin's character.

Pedro Alonso's return to the role required careful consideration of character continuity. Set before Berlin's terminal illness diagnosis in Money Heist, the series portrays him at the peak of his powers - more vibrant, more arrogant, and more dangerously charming. Alonso worked closely with the writers to ensure his performance honored the established character while exploring new dimensions.

The new ensemble cast was carefully selected to complement Berlin's personality while bringing their own unique energy. Michelle Jenner's Keila represents the technological expertise needed for modern heists, while Begoña Vargas's Cameron brings physical skill and emotional complexity. The chemistry between the cast members was developed through intensive rehearsals and workshops.

The Parisian setting influenced every aspect of production design, from the elegant auction house where the heist takes place to Berlin's luxurious apartment with its stunning views of the Eiffel Tower. The cinematography captures the romantic, almost dreamlike quality of Paris while maintaining the tension and urgency of a high-stakes heist.

The series faced the challenge of balancing fan expectations with creative innovation. While maintaining signature Money Heist elements like the strategic planning sequences and unexpected twists, Berlin carves its own identity with a more romantic, character-focused narrative that explores love, obsession, and the price of genius.
